# NCA DCC-1:2022 — Data Cybersecurity Controls

## At a glance

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Issuer | National Cybersecurity Authority (NCA), Kingdom of Saudi Arabia |
| Document | Data Cybersecurity Controls (DCC-1:2022) |
| TLP / Classification | White / Public |
| Binding language | Arabic (English version is reference only) |
| Status | Mandatory for all in-scope entities |
| Relationship to ECC | DCC is an **extension** to ECC. ECC compliance is a **prerequisite** — DCC compliance cannot be achieved without continuous ECC compliance |
| Compliance evaluation | NCA self-assessments and/or external assessments |

---

## Purpose

Provides the minimum cybersecurity requirements to protect data throughout its **entire lifecycle** — creation, storage, sharing, use, and disposal — across both physical and digital forms, structured (databases, tables) and unstructured (documents, records).

---

## Applicability (DCC Scope of Work)

Applies to:
- Government agencies in KSA (ministries, authorities, establishments)
- Their affiliated companies and entities
- Private-sector entities owning, operating, or hosting Critical National Infrastructures (CNIs)

Covers **all forms** of physical and digital data, including structured (databases, tables) and unstructured (documents, records).

NCA strongly encourages all other KSA entities to adopt these controls voluntarily.

---

## Data classification levels

DCC controls are tiered against four classification levels derived from SDAIA/NDMO tools. Controls specify **which level triggers which requirement**:

| Level | Colour in document | Sensitivity |
|-------|--------------------|-------------|
| **Public** (عام) | Green | Lowest |
| **Confidential** (سري) | Yellow | Medium |
| **Secret** (سري للغاية) | Red | High |
| **Top Secret** | Dark red | Highest |

> **How to read the controls:** Each control row has four columns — Public / Confidential / Secret / Top Secret. A tick (✓) in a column means the control **is mandatory** for data at that classification level. A blank means it does not apply at that level. Always check the applicable column(s) for the entity's data holdings.

### 1-1 Periodical Cybersecurity Review and Audit

Extends ECC control 1-8. Review frequencies are classification-driven:

| Control | Obligation | Public / Confidential | Secret / Top Secret |
|---------|-----------|----------------------|---------------------|
| 1-1-1 | Internal review of DCC implementation | At least annually | At least annually |
| 1-1-2 | Independent external audit of DCC | At least every 2 years | At least annually |

**Audit hot spot:** The independent external audit requirement for Secret/Top Secret data (annual, not biennial) is frequently missed.

|-------------|-----------|-------------------|

| 1-2-1-2 | Signed agreement not to use social media, personal communication apps, or personal cloud storage to create, store, or share organisation data — except via authority-approved secure communications | Confidential, Secret, Top Secret |

## Domain 2 — Cybersecurity Defense (7 subdomains)

| Ref | Subdomain |
|-----|-----------|
| 2-1 | Identity and Access Management |
| 2-2 | Information System and Information Processing Facilities Protection |
| 2-3 | Mobile Devices Security |
| 2-4 | Data and Information Protection |
| 2-5 | Cryptography |
| 2-6 | Secure Data Disposal |
| 2-7 | Cybersecurity for Printers, Scanners and Copy Machines |

### 2-1 Identity and Access Management

Extends ECC control 2-2-3. Additional requirements (main control 2-1-1):

| Sub-control | Obligation | Applicable levels |
|-------------|-----------|-------------------|
| **2-1-1-1** | Restrict data access, viewing, and sharing to the **minimum number of personnel**, limited to lists of privileges restricted to **Saudi-national employees**, unless exempted by the Authorising Official. Lists must be approved by the Authorising Official. | **Secret, Top Secret** |
| 2-1-1-2 | Prohibit sharing of approved privilege lists with unauthorised persons | Confidential, Secret, Top Secret |
| 2-1-2 | Manage identities and access rights to view data using **Privileged Access Management (PAM)** systems | Confidential, Secret, Top Secret |
| 2-1-3 | Review approved lists of privileges and actual privileges used to handle data (extends ECC 2-2-3-5): at least annually for Public/Confidential; **at least every 3 months** for Secret/Top Secret | All levels (frequency varies) |

> **Critical note on 2-1-1-1:** For Secret and Top Secret data, access is restricted to Saudi nationals unless the Authorising Official grants an exemption. This is separate from the ECC-2 staffing Saudization requirement (1-2-2) and applies specifically to **data access privileges**.

### 2-2 Information System and Information Processing Facilities Protection

Extends ECC control 2-3-3. Additional requirements (main control 2-2-1):

| Sub-control | Obligation | Applicable levels |
|-------------|-----------|-------------------|
| **2-2-1-1** | Apply security patches and updates from the time of announcement: **at least every month** for Public/Confidential; **immediately** for Secret/Top Secret | All levels (urgency varies) |
| 2-2-1-2 | Review security configuration and hardening of systems used to handle data: at least annually for Public/Confidential; **at least every 6 months** for Secret/Top Secret | All levels (frequency varies) |
| 2-2-1-3 | Review and harden default configuration (e.g. default passwords, backgrounds) of technology assets used to handle data | All four levels |
| **2-2-1-4** | Disable Print Screen / Screen Capture features on devices that create or process documents | **Secret, Top Secret** |

### 2-3 Mobile Devices Security

Extends ECC control 2-6-3. Additional requirements (main control 2-3-1):

| Sub-control | Obligation | Applicable levels |
|-------------|-----------|-------------------|
| 2-3-1-1 | Centrally manage organisation-owned mobile devices using **MDM** system and activate remote-wipe feature | All four levels |
| 2-3-1-2 | Centrally manage BYOD devices using MDM and activate remote-wipe feature | Public, Confidential only — **use of BYOD devices is prohibited for Secret and Top Secret data** |

> **BYOD prohibition:** For Secret and Top Secret classification levels, BYOD devices may not be used at all. MDM management of BYODs is only relevant at Public and Confidential levels.

### 2-4 Data and Information Protection

Extends ECC control 2-7-3. Additional requirements (main control 2-4-1):

| Sub-control | Obligation | Applicable levels |
|-------------|-----------|-------------------|
| **2-4-1-1** | Apply **Watermark** to entire document when creating, storing, printing, and displaying on screen and on each copy, traceable to the user or device level | **Secret, Top Secret** |
| 2-4-1-2 | Deploy **Data Leakage Prevention (DLP)** and **Rights Management** technologies | Confidential, Secret, Top Secret |
| 2-4-1-3 | Prohibit use of data in any environment other than production, except after risk assessment and applying controls such as **data masking or data scrambling** | Confidential, Secret, Top Secret |
| 2-4-1-4 | Use **brand protection service** to protect the organisation's identity from impersonation | All four levels |

### 2-5 Cryptography

Extends ECC control 2-8-3. References **National Cryptographic Standards (NCS-1:2020)**. Additional requirements (main control 2-5-1):

| Sub-control | Obligation | Applicable levels |
|-------------|-----------|-------------------|
| **2-5-1-1** | Use secure and up-to-date cryptographic methods/algorithms for creating, storing, transmitting data and for overall network communication, meeting the **"advanced level"** in NCS-1:2020 | **Secret, Top Secret** |
| **2-5-1-2** | Use secure and up-to-date cryptographic methods/algorithms meeting the **"moderate level"** in NCS-1:2020 | **Confidential** |

> Public data has no DCC cryptographic requirement beyond ECC-2:2024 baseline.

### 2-6 Secure Data Disposal

This is a **new subdomain** in DCC with no direct ECC equivalent. All requirements under main control 2-6-1:

| Sub-control | Obligation | Applicable levels |
|-------------|-----------|-------------------|
| 2-6-1-1 | Identify technologies, tools, and procedures for implementing secure data disposal according to classification level | Confidential, Secret, Top Secret |
| 2-6-1-2 | When storage media is no longer needed, securely dispose using tools/procedures identified in 2-6-1-1 | Confidential, Secret, Top Secret |
| 2-6-1-3 | When storage media is re-used, data must be **securely erased** (secure erasure) in a manner it cannot be recovered | Confidential, Secret, Top Secret |
| 2-6-1-4 | Verify that disposal and erasure operations in 2-6-1-2 and 2-6-1-3 have been carried out correctly | Confidential, Secret, Top Secret |
| 2-6-1-5 | Keep a **record** of all secure data disposal and erasure operations | Confidential, Secret, Top Secret |
| 2-6-2 | Review implementation of secure data disposal: **at least annually** for Confidential; **at least every 6 months** for Secret/Top Secret | Confidential, Secret, Top Secret |

### 2-7 Cybersecurity for Printers, Scanners and Copy Machines

This is a **new subdomain** in DCC with no direct ECC equivalent. Applies from Confidential level upwards.

| Control | Obligation | Applicable levels |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| 2-7-1 | Define, document, and approve cybersecurity requirements for printers, scanners, and copy machines | Confidential, Secret, Top Secret |
| 2-7-2 | Implement the requirements | Confidential, Secret, Top Secret |
| 2-7-3-1 | Disable the **temporary storage feature** | Secret, Top Secret |
| 2-7-3-2 | Enable **authentication** on centralised printers, scanners, and copy machines and require it before usage | Secret, Top Secret |
| 2-7-3-3 | Securely retain logs of printer/scanner/copy machine usage for **not less than 12 months** | Secret, Top Secret |
| 2-7-3-4 | Enable and protect **CCTV logs** monitoring areas where centralised printers/scanners/copy machines are located | Secret, Top Secret |
| 2-7-3-5 | Use **cross-shredding devices** to securely dispose of documents when no longer needed | Secret, Top Secret |
| 2-7-4 | Review implementation: at least every 3 years (Confidential); **at least annually** (Secret/Top Secret) | Confidential, Secret, Top Secret |

## Domain 3 — Third-Party and Cloud Computing Cybersecurity (1 subdomain)

| Ref | Subdomain |
|-----|-----------|
| 3-1 | Third-Party Cybersecurity |

### 3-1 Third-Party Cybersecurity

Extends ECC subdomain 4-1. Two tracks:

**Track A — General third parties (3-1-1):** Additional requirements on top of ECC 4-1 for all third parties with access to organisational data:

| Sub-control | Obligation | Applicable levels |
|-------------|-----------|-------------------|
| 3-1-1-1 | Screen or vet third-party employees who have access to the data | Secret, Top Secret |
| 3-1-1-2 | Require contractual commitment by third parties to **securely dispose** of the organisation's data at contract end or termination, with evidence provided to the organisation | Confidential, Secret, Top Secret |
| 3-1-1-3 | **Document all data sharing operations** with third parties, including data sharing justification | Confidential, Secret, Top Secret |
| **3-1-1-4** | When transferring data **outside the Kingdom**, verify the capability of the foreign hosting organisation to safeguard data, obtain **Authorising Official approval**, and comply with related laws and regulations | Confidential, Secret, Top Secret |
| 3-1-1-5 | Require third parties to **notify the organisation immediately** in case of a cybersecurity incident that may affect shared or created data | Confidential, Secret, Top Secret |
| 3-1-1-6 | Reclassify data to the least level needed to achieve the objective before sharing with third parties, using **data masking or data scrambling** | Confidential, Secret, Top Secret |

**Track B — Consultancy services on high-sensitivity strategic national projects (3-1-2):** Additional controls beyond Track A for consultancy services (not professional cybersecurity services) working on high-sensitivity strategic projects at national level:

| Sub-control | Obligation | Applicable levels |
|-------------|-----------|-------------------|
| 3-1-2-1 | Screen or vet consultancy service employees with data access | Secret, Top Secret |
| 3-1-2-2 | Require NDA and secure data disposal commitment from consultancy, with disposal evidence | Confidential, Secret, Top Secret |
| 3-1-2-3 | Document all data sharing operations with consultancy, including justification | Confidential, Secret, Top Secret |
| 3-1-2-4 | Require immediate notification in case of a cybersecurity incident affecting shared data | Confidential, Secret, Top Secret |
| 3-1-2-5 | Reclassify data to the least necessary level before sharing, using masking/scrambling | Confidential, Secret, Top Secret |
| **3-1-2-6** | Dedicate a **closed room** for consultancy staff to perform their work, and provide dedicated organisation-owned devices for data sharing and processing | Secret, Top Secret |
| **3-1-2-7** | Activate access control system allowing **only authorised access** to the closed room | Secret, Top Secret |
| **3-1-2-8** | **Prevent** carrying devices, storage media, and documents outside the closed room, and prevent entry of any other electronic devices | Secret, Top Secret |

> **Consultancy definition (from DCC Terms):** Professional advisory services (e.g. Digital Transformation Consultancy, strategy and regulation analysis) where consultants review client business data. Does **not** include professional cybersecurity services (which are separately defined as NCA-certified/licensed providers).

## DCC relationship to ECC — how the extension model works

DCC is **additive**, not standalone. The dependency is explicit:

- 9 ECC subdomains have additional DCC controls layered on top (e.g. ECC 2-2-3 → DCC 2-1, ECC 2-6-3 → DCC 2-3)
- 20 ECC subdomains have no DCC addition (ECC controls apply unchanged)
- 2 subdomains are **new to DCC** with no ECC parent: 2-6 (Secure Data Disposal) and 2-7 (Printers/Scanners/Copy Machines)

This means assessors must always run ECC controls first, then apply the DCC overlay where a data classification level triggers additional requirements.

## Audit hot spots — triage list

1. **Saudi-national access restriction (2-1-1-1):** For Secret/Top Secret data, access lists must be limited to Saudi nationals and Authorising-Official-approved exceptions. Rarely fully implemented.
2. **Emergency patch timelines (2-2-1-1):** Patches must be applied **immediately** for Secret/Top Secret systems (not monthly). Confirm change-management process can support this.
3. **BYOD prohibition (2-3-1-2):** BYOD devices are prohibited for Secret/Top Secret data. Any BYOD MDM enrolment for those classifications is non-compliant.
4. **DLP + Rights Management deployment (2-4-1-2):** Both technologies required from Confidential level. DLP alone is insufficient.
5. **Watermarking (2-4-1-1):** Required for Secret/Top Secret; must be traceable to user or device. Often absent or not device-traceable.
6. **Data masking before non-prod use (2-4-1-3):** Confidential data in test/dev environments is non-compliant without masking/scrambling and prior risk assessment.
7. **Cryptographic standard level (2-5-1):** Confidential = NCS-1:2020 moderate level; Secret/Top Secret = NCS-1:2020 advanced level. Many entities apply a single undifferentiated standard.
8. **Secure disposal records (2-6-1-5):** Records of all disposal and erasure operations must be kept; verification evidence (2-6-1-4) is frequently missing.
9. **Printer/scanner controls (2-7):** Entirely new subdomain; temporary storage disabled, authentication required, 12-month usage logs, CCTV, and cross-shredder all required at Secret/Top Secret. Rarely addressed.
10. **Cross-border transfer (3-1-1-4):** Requires capability verification of foreign host, Authorising Official approval, and legal compliance. Each transfer must be documented (3-1-1-3).
11. **Consultancy closed-room controls (3-1-2-6, 3-1-2-7, 3-1-2-8):** Dedicated room, access control, and device prohibition for consultancy access to Secret/Top Secret data. Common gap in organisations using external strategy consultants.
12. **Independent audit frequency (1-1-2):** Annual external audit required for Secret/Top Secret data. Many entities rely on biennial internal reviews.

## Evidence expectations

| Control area | Evidence an auditor will request |
|-------------|----------------------------------|
| Access control (2-1) | Approved privilege lists, Saudi-national verification, PAM configuration, review records |
| Patching (2-2-1-1) | Patch deployment logs with timestamps; immediate deployment evidence for Secret/Top Secret |
| DLP (2-4-1-2) | DLP policy, deployment architecture, tuning evidence across email/endpoint/cloud vectors |
| Watermarking (2-4-1-1) | Sample documents; system configuration showing user/device traceability |

| Disposal (2-6-1) | Disposal/erasure records, verification evidence, review logs |
| Printers (2-7) | Configuration screenshots, authentication policy, usage logs (12 months), CCTV evidence |
| Third-party (3-1) | Data sharing agreements, disposal commitments, data sharing register, transfer approvals |
| Consultancy (3-1-2) | Room access logs, device allocation records, NDA copies, disposal evidence |

## Common pitfalls

- Applying uniform access controls regardless of classification level — DCC requires **differentiated** controls by level.
- Treating BYOD MDM management as sufficient for Secret/Top Secret data — BYOD is prohibited at those levels.
- Implementing DLP without Rights Management — both are required from Confidential level upwards.
- Applying only the ECC cryptographic baseline to all data — DCC requires NCS-1:2020 moderate/advanced level based on classification.
- Missing disposal records — disposal procedures may exist, but verification evidence and logs are frequently absent.
- Treating printer/scanner security as a physical security matter rather than a DCC compliance matter.
- Cross-border transfers managed informally without Authorising Official approval documentation.
- Conflating the consultancy-services track (3-1-2) with general third-party controls (3-1-1) — the closed-room and device prohibitions in 3-1-2 are additional and apply specifically to consultancy.

## Key definitions (from DCC Terms)

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **DLP Technologies** | Technologies protecting sensitive data from unauthorised disclosure — covering data at rest (on volumes), in use (on devices/servers), and in transit (over network) |
| **MDM System** | Technical system to manage, monitor, and protect mobile devices by applying cybersecurity policies |
| **Rights Management Technologies** | Technologies protecting sensitive data from unauthorised disclosure and limiting processing to assigned privileges |
| **Consulting Services** | Professional advisory services (e.g. digital transformation, strategy analysis) where consultants review business data. Does **not** include professional cybersecurity services |
| **Professional Cybersecurity Services** | NCA-certified/licensed services for vulnerability assessment, incident response, risk assessment |
| **Managed Services** | NCA-certified/licensed subscription services offloading IT and cybersecurity operations (e.g. managed SOC, managed IT) |

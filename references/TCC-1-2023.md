## Purpose

The TCC establishes the minimum cybersecurity requirements to enable national organisations to perform telework in a secure manner. Issued in recognition that increasing dependence on telework increases cyber threats and risks, requiring cybersecurity requirements to reduce these threats and risks alongside the ECC baseline.

Stated objectives:
1. Enable an organisation's work to be performed remotely in a secure manner and adapt to changes in the business environment and in telework systems.
2. Enhance the organisation's cybersecurity capabilities and resilience where telework is exposed to cyber threats.
3. Contribute to raising the level of cybersecurity at the national level.

---

## Applicability (TCC Scope of Work)

**In-scope entities (mandatory):**
- Government organisations in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia: ministries, authorities, establishments, and similar bodies
- Companies and entities affiliated with government organisations
- Private-sector organisations owning, operating, or hosting Critical National Infrastructure (CNIs)

All in-scope entities are collectively referred to as "The Organisation" in the TCC.

**Voluntary adoption:** NCA strongly encourages all other KSA organisations to leverage these controls as best practice.

**Trigger:** Every organisation that **allows telework** must comply with all applicable controls.

**Statement of Applicability note:** Controls in subdomain **3-1** (Cloud Computing and Hosting Cybersecurity) apply only to organisations currently using or planning to use cloud computing and hosting services.

### 1-3 Cybersecurity Awareness and Training Program

**Objective:** Ensure personnel are aware of their cybersecurity responsibilities and have the essential cybersecurity awareness; and that personnel are provided with the required cybersecurity training, skills, and credentials to accomplish their responsibilities and protect the organisation's information and technology assets.

## Audit hot spots — triage list

Ranked by frequency in TCC gap assessments:

1. **No MDM for mobile devices and BYODs (2-5-1-1):** Central MDM management is mandatory for all mobile devices and BYODs used for telework. Absence of MDM or MDM enrolled but not enforcing policy is the most common TCC gap.
2. **Mobile device patches not monthly (2-5-1-2):** TCC requires monthly mobile patching — stricter than the ECC general patch requirement. Quarterly cycles applied universally are non-compliant.
3. **Telework system patches not quarterly (2-3-1-1):** Telework infrastructure (VPN gateways, remote desktop servers, collaboration tools) must be patched at least every three months.
4. **Vulnerability assessment not quarterly (2-9-1-1):** Annual vulnerability scans meeting ECC requirements do not satisfy TCC's quarterly cadence for telework systems.
5. **No User Behaviour Analytics (UBA) on telework (2-11-1-2):** UBA is an explicit TCC requirement; generic SIEM log collection without UBA use cases is insufficient.
6. **Telework monitoring not 24/7 (2-11-1-3, 2-11-1-4):** Business-hours-only monitoring is non-compliant; the TCC requires around-the-clock monitoring explicitly including access from outside KSA.
7. **Log retention below 12 months for telework systems (2-11-2):** VPN/remote-access appliance logs typically retain 30–90 days; logs must be forwarded to SIEM with 12-month retention.
8. **Pen test scope excludes telework components (2-10-1-1):** Penetration tests covering internet-facing web services often exclude VPN gateway, MDM platform, remote access broker, and virtual collaboration infrastructure.
9. **Cloud-hosted telework systems located outside KSA (3-1-1-1):** VPN concentrators, virtual meeting infrastructure, or remote desktop services on international cloud regions violate the explicit KSA-hosting requirement.
10. **Concurrent login not restricted (2-2-1-2):** The explicit prohibition on simultaneous remote access from multiple devices for the same user is often not implemented in standard VPN/IAM configurations.
11. **Default/hard-coded passwords not removed (2-3-1-3):** Factory defaults on VPN appliances, collaboration tools, and remote desktop components often remain unchanged.
12. **Classified data permitted via telework not formally identified (2-6-1-1):** No documented classification scope for telework; DLP rules have no basis.
13. **Encryption below NCS Advanced level (2-7-1-1):** Telework connections must use the Advanced level of NCS 1:2020; deprecated cipher suites (TLS 1.0/1.1, weak key lengths) are non-compliant.
14. **Telework risk register entries absent or not reviewed annually (1-2-1-3):** Generic enterprise risk register without telework-specific entries; no evidence of annual review.
15. **Incident response plan not adapted for telework context (2-12-1-1):** Plans not updated to address communication and response when responders themselves are working remotely.

## Common pitfalls

- **Applying ECC quarterly patch cadence to mobile devices:** TCC 2-5-1-2 explicitly requires monthly mobile device patches. Entities that apply a single quarterly patching cycle to all assets are non-compliant for mobile.
- **MDM enrolled but not enforcing policy:** Devices appear in MDM in reporting-only mode. Enrolment alone does not satisfy 2-5-1-1 — policy profiles must be deployed and enforced.
- **VPN appliance logs treated as meeting the 12-month retention requirement:** Appliance-local logs overwrite within weeks. TCC 2-11-2 requires centralised SIEM forwarding with 12-month retention.
- **Pen test scope defined as "internet-facing services" without naming telework infrastructure:** VPN gateways, MDM platforms, and remote collaboration tools are telework systems — explicit inclusion in scope document is required.
- **DLP deployed without completing the classified data identification step:** Many entities deploy DLP tools without first completing 2-6-1-1 (identifying what classified data is used via telework). Auditors will find the identification step missing and the DLP rules therefore without basis.
- **NCS Advanced level not verified:** Organisations confirm "encryption is in use" without checking cipher suites against NCS 1:2020 Advanced requirements. Deprecated protocols in legacy VPN configurations are a frequent finding.
- **Telework systems hosted internationally on assumption data localisation no longer applies:** Control 3-1-1-1 explicitly requires telework system hosting inside KSA — this obligation is in TCC, independent of the ECC-1 deletion of 4-2-3-3.
- **Awareness programme covers ECC topics but not the 8 TCC-specific topics (1-3-1-1 to 1-3-1-8):** Generic cybersecurity awareness without telework-specific content (home network security, public device avoidance, physical security at the home workspace) does not satisfy TCC.
- **Concurrent login restriction implemented in identity provider only:** VPN gateway, remote desktop broker, and application portals must each enforce concurrent session restrictions; identity-provider-only enforcement can be bypassed via direct protocol access.

## Key definitions (Appendix B — confirmed from source)

| Term | TCC Definition |
|------|----------------|
| **Telework Systems** | Any technical systems, means or tools and related components used by the organisation to enable employees to perform their job duties in a place other than the official workplace. Examples: virtual meeting systems, collaboration systems, file sharing, VPN, remote access systems, and other systems used in the work environment |
| **User Behaviour Analytics (UBA)** | The process of tracking and collecting user data, analysing it, and defining patterns of user activity to detect harmful or unusual behaviours |
| **Data Leakage Prevention** | Technologies used to protect sensitive data from unauthorised disclosure and to prevent its circulation outside the organisation in any form — whether at-rest (stored on volumes), in-use (on user devices or servers), or in-transit (in movement via the network) |
| **Distributed Denial of Service Attack (DDoS)** | An attempt to disable the system and make its services unavailable by sending many requests from more than one source at the same time |
| **Mobile Device Management (MDM) System** | A technical system used to manage, monitor, and protect mobile devices by applying cybersecurity policies |

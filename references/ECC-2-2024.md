# NCA ECC-2:2024 — Essential Cybersecurity Controls

## At a glance

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Issuer | National Cybersecurity Authority (NCA), Kingdom of Saudi Arabia |
| Document | Essential Cybersecurity Controls (ECC-2:2024) |
| Supersedes | ECC-1:2018 |
| TLP / Classification | White / Public |

| Status | Mandatory for all in-scope entities |
| Compliance mechanism | Self-assessment + periodic compliance tool reports + field audit visits (ECC-2:2024 Assessment and Compliance Tool) |

---

## Applicability (ECC Scope of Work)

Applies to:

- Government agencies in KSA (ministries, authorities, establishments, and similar bodies)
- Their affiliated companies and entities — inside **and** outside the Kingdom
- Private-sector entities owning, operating, or hosting Critical National Infrastructure (CNIs)

NCA strongly encourages all other KSA entities to voluntarily adopt the controls as a baseline.

### Statement of Applicability note

Not every control applies to every entity. Key example:

- Subdomain **4-2** (Cloud Computing and Hosting Cybersecurity) is binding **only** on entities currently using or planning to use cloud / hosting services.

## Domain 1 — Cybersecurity Governance (10 subdomains)

| Ref | Subdomain |
|-----|-----------|
| 1-1 | Cybersecurity Strategy |
| 1-2 | Cybersecurity Management |
| 1-3 | Cybersecurity Policies and Procedures |
| 1-4 | Cybersecurity Roles and Responsibilities |
| 1-5 | Cybersecurity Risk Management |
| 1-6 | Cybersecurity in Information and Technology Project Management |
| 1-7 | Compliance with Cybersecurity Standards, Laws and Regulations |
| 1-8 | Periodical Cybersecurity Review and Audit |
| 1-9 | Cybersecurity in Human Resources |
| 1-10 | Cybersecurity Awareness and Training Program |

### Key controls — Domain 1

**1-1 (Strategy):** Strategy must be documented, approved, and supported by the Authorized Official (head of entity or delegate). Action plan required (1-1-2). Reviewed at planned intervals (1-1-3).

**1-2 (Management):**
- 1-2-1: Cybersecurity department must be **independent** from IT/Communications (as per High Order 37140, 14/08/1438H). Recommended to report directly to entity head.
- **1-2-2 (critical — Saudization):** All cybersecurity positions shall be filled with **full-time and qualified Saudi** cybersecurity professionals. *(Changed from ECC-1, which only required the CISO and supervisory positions to be Saudi.)*
- 1-2-3: Cybersecurity supervisory committee required, with cybersecurity department head as a member.

**1-3 (Policies):** Policies must be documented, approved by Authorized Official, communicated internally, supported by technical security standards (firewall, DB, OS hardening, etc.), and reviewed at planned intervals (1-3-4).

**1-5 (Risk Management):** Risk assessment is **mandatory** at minimum in four scenarios (1-5-3): early stage of technology projects; before major infrastructure changes; during planning to obtain third-party services; before release of new technology services/products.

**1-6 (Project Management):** Secure coding standards, trusted/licensed software tools, compliance testing, secure integration, and pre-launch hardening review are all minimum requirements for software development projects (1-6-3).

**1-7 (Compliance):** Only one control remains: if nationally approved international agreements include cybersecurity requirements (e.g., SWIFT, PCI), the entity must identify and comply. *(The previous blanket national-law compliance control was deleted as redundant given regulatory maturity.)*

**1-8 (Audit):** Independent review required (not just internal cybersecurity team). Results must include scope, observations, recommendations, corrective actions, and remediation plans — reported to the supervisory committee and the Authorized Official (1-8-3).

**1-9 (HR):** Pre-employment screening required for cybersecurity and privileged-access positions (1-9-3-2). Powers must be revoked **immediately** upon employment end/termination (1-9-5).

**1-10 (Awareness):** Training must be role-differentiated (1-10-4): cybersecurity staff, software/IT asset staff, and executive/supervisory positions each have separate requirements.

## Domain 2 — Cybersecurity Defense (15 subdomains)

| Ref | Subdomain |
|-----|-----------|
| 2-1 | Asset Management |
| 2-2 | Identity and Access Management |
| 2-3 | Information Systems and Information Processing Facilities Protection |
| 2-4 | Email Protection |
| 2-5 | Network Security Management |
| 2-6 | Mobile Devices Security |
| 2-7 | Data and Information Protection |
| 2-8 | Cryptography |
| 2-9 | Backup and Recovery Management |
| 2-10 | Vulnerability Management |
| 2-11 | Penetration Testing |
| 2-12 | Cybersecurity Event Logs and Monitoring Management |
| 2-13 | Cybersecurity Incident and Threat Management |
| 2-14 | Physical Security |
| 2-15 | Web Application Security |

### Key controls — Domain 2

**2-1 (Asset Management):** Assets must be classified, labeled, and handled per regulatory requirements (2-1-5). Acceptable-use policy must be documented, approved, communicated, and implemented.

**2-2 (IAM):** Minimum requirements (2-2-3):
- Single-factor authentication based on username and password (2-2-3-1) — ECC-1 did not explicitly name "single-factor"
- **MFA** (2-2-3-2): required for **remote access AND privileged accounts**, with authentication factors and techniques defined by impact assessment of failure/bypass. *(Expanded from ECC-1 which said "remote access" only.)*
- Least Privilege + Need-to-Know/Need-to-Use + Segregation of Duties (2-2-3-3)
- Privileged access management (2-2-3-4)
- Periodic review of identities and access rights (2-2-3-5)

**2-3 (System Protection):** Minimum requirements: anti-malware on workstations and servers using advanced technologies (2-3-3-1); strict restriction on external storage media (2-3-3-2); patch management (2-3-3-3); centralized clock synchronisation with a SASO-approved source (2-3-3-4).

**2-4 (Email):** Minimum requirements:
- Phishing/spam filtering with advanced techniques (2-4-3-1)
- MFA for remote and webmail access (2-4-3-2) — impact-assessment-based factors
- Email archiving and backup (2-4-3-3)
- APT/zero-day protection (2-4-3-4)
- **SPF + DKIM + DMARC** domain validation required (2-4-3-5) *(ECC-1 required only SPF.)*

**2-5 (Network):** Minimum requirements: network segmentation with firewall and defense-in-depth (2-5-3-1); isolation of production from dev/test (2-5-3-2); secure browsing restrictions (2-5-3-3); wireless security with encryption (2-5-3-4); port/protocol restriction (2-5-3-5); IPS (2-5-3-6); DNS security (2-5-3-7); APT/zero-day protection on internet browsing channel (2-5-3-8); **DDoS protection (2-5-3-9)** *(new addition in ECC-2).*

**2-6 (Mobile / BYOD):** Data separation and encryption required on devices and BYODs (2-6-3-1); remote wipe mandatory on device loss or employment termination (2-6-3-3).

**2-7 (Data Protection):** Protection must be implemented **based on classification level** (2-7-2). *(Data localization requirements moved to NDMO — see §Important deletions.)*

**2-8 (Cryptography):** Requirements must meet **NCA National Cryptographic Standards** (2-8-3). Appropriate level determined by data/system/network sensitivity plus risk assessment. Mandatory: approved cryptographic systems (2-8-3-1); key management (2-8-3-2); encryption of data **in-transit and at-rest** per classification (2-8-3-3).

**2-9 (Backup):** Minimum: coverage of critical assets (2-9-3-1); ability for **quick recovery** post-incident (2-9-3-2); periodic recovery testing (2-9-3-3).

**2-10 (Vulnerability Management):** Minimum: periodic assessment and detection (2-10-3-1); severity-based classification (2-10-3-2); remediation based on risk (2-10-3-3); patch management with pre-production testing before applying (2-10-3-4); subscription to trusted vulnerability feeds (2-10-3-5).

**2-11 (Penetration Testing):** Scope must cover all externally provided services (internet-facing): infrastructure, websites, web apps, mobile apps (smartphone and tablet), email, and remote access (2-11-3-1). Testing must be conducted periodically (2-11-3-2).

**2-12 (SIEM/Log Management):** Minimum:
- Event logs activated for critical information assets (2-12-3-1)
- Event logs activated for critical/privileged accounts and remote-access events (2-12-3-2)
- SIEM techniques identified for log collection (2-12-3-3)
- **Continuous monitoring** (2-12-3-4)
- **Log retention at least 12 months** (2-12-3-5)

**2-13 (Incident Management):** Minimum: incident response plans and escalation procedures (2-13-3-1); incident classification (2-13-3-2); **reporting to NCA** (2-13-3-3); sharing incident notifications, threat intelligence, and penetration indicators with NCA (2-13-3-4); threat intelligence feed collection (2-13-3-5). Reference: High Order 37140, 14/08/1438H.

**2-14 (Physical Security):** Minimum: authorised access controls for data centres, DR centres, server rooms, network rooms, equipment supply areas (2-14-3-1); CCTV and access logs (2-14-3-2); protection of log information (2-14-3-3); secure destruction/re-use of assets containing classified data (2-14-3-4); physical security of devices inside and outside facilities (2-14-3-5).

**2-15 (Web Application Security):** Minimum: WAF (2-15-3-1); multi-tier architecture (2-15-3-2); secure protocols e.g. HTTPS (2-15-3-3); secure-use policy for users (2-15-3-4); **user authentication** with factors/techniques defined by impact assessment of bypass (2-15-3-5). *(Changed from ECC-1 "MFA for users' access" to a risk-based authentication approach.)*

## Domain 4 — Third-Party and Cloud Computing Cybersecurity (2 subdomains)

| Ref | Subdomain |
|-----|-----------|
| 4-1 | Third-Party Cybersecurity |
| 4-2 | Cloud Computing and Hosting Cybersecurity |

### Key controls — Domain 4

**4-1 (Third-Party):**

Minimum contractual requirements for all third parties whose impairment could affect entity data or services (4-1-2):
- Non-disclosure clauses and secure data removal upon end of service (4-1-2-1)
- Incident communication procedures (4-1-2-2)
- Obligation to comply with entity's cybersecurity requirements and applicable laws (4-1-2-3)

Additional requirements for IT/cybersecurity outsourcing or managed services (4-1-3):
- Pre-contract cybersecurity risk assessment (4-1-3-1)
- **4-1-3-2 (KSA residency — critical):** Cybersecurity managed service centres using **remote access** for monitoring and operations must be **fully located in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia.** *(Key for managed SOC procurement decisions.)*

**4-2 (Cloud / Hosting):** Applicable only where the entity uses or plans to use cloud/hosting services. Requirements supplement applicable controls from Domains 1, 2, 3, and Subdomain 4-1.

Minimum:
- **4-2-3-1:** Cloud/hosting providers must protect entity data in accordance with its **classification level** and return data in a usable format upon service completion. *(ECC-1 required the entity to classify data before hosting; ECC-2 explicitly places the ongoing protection obligation on the provider.)*
- 4-2-3-2: Separation of entity's virtual environment (including virtual servers) from other tenants.

## Important deletions from ECC-1:2018

| Deleted item | Reason | Where it now lives |
|-------------|--------|-------------------|
| Main Domain 5 — Industrial Control Systems Cybersecurity | Dedicated standalone framework issued | OTCC-1:2022 |
| Sub-control 4-2-3-3 — Entity's information must be hosted and stored inside KSA | Data-localisation mandate transferred | NDMO, under SDAIA |
| Sub-control 2-7-3 — Data ownership, classification/labelling, privacy | Privacy and data management mandate transferred | NDMO Standards / PDPL |
| Control 1-7-1 — Comply with national cybersecurity laws | Redundant given regulatory maturity | Deleted |
| Privacy definition | Privacy mandate transferred | NDMO / PDPL |

> **Critical practice note:** Deleting 4-2-3-3 from ECC does **not** mean data localisation no longer applies to KSA entities. Entities must separately check NDMO Data Management Standards, PDPL Implementing Regulations, and any sector-specific requirements. Always route data-localisation questions to NDMO guidance before advising on cloud architecture.

## Audit hot spots — triage list

Ranked by frequency in NCA gap assessments:

1. **Saudization (1-2-2):** All cybersecurity positions must be full-time qualified Saudi nationals. Contractor arrangements and non-Saudi operational staff are non-compliant.
2. **Strategy board approval (1-1-1):** Documented strategy with Authorized Official endorsement; action plan evidenced.
3. **MFA coverage (2-2-3-2, 2-4-3-2, 2-15-3-5):** Remote access and privileged accounts; impact assessment required to justify factor choice.
4. **SIEM + continuous monitoring (2-12):** Log scope, use-case coverage, 12-month retention, and 24/7 alerting.
5. **Vulnerability management SLAs (2-10):** Classification, remediation timelines, and subscription to trusted feeds.
6. **Penetration testing scope (2-11):** All internet-facing services including mobile applications.
7. **Email — SPF + DKIM + DMARC (2-4-3-5):** All three required; SPF-only deployments are non-compliant.
8. **DDoS protection (2-5-3-9):** New control; often absent in legacy environments.
9. **KSA-resident managed SOC (4-1-3-2):** Offshore SOC operations are non-compliant regardless of data-sovereignty posture.
10. **Third-party contractual clauses (4-1-2):** Legacy contracts frequently lack NDA and incident-notification obligations.
11. **Cloud data protection (4-2-3-1):** Classification-level protection obligation on provider; shared-responsibility matrix and contract clause required.
12. **Incident reporting to NCA (2-13-3-3, 2-13-3-4):** Documented procedures and NCA contact channels must exist and be tested.
13. **Log retention 12 months (2-12-3-5):** Verify SIEM storage capacity; many deployments retain 30–90 days.
14. **Awareness programme (1-10):** Role-differentiated content; completion tracking mandatory.
15. **HR — immediate access revocation (1-9-5):** Leaver process often breaks down; spot-check against AD/IDM off-boarding records.

## Evidence expectations (per control)

An NCA auditor will typically request:

- Approved policy or procedure (version date and Authorized Official signature)
- Implementation records (change tickets, configuration exports, SIEM screenshots, scan reports)
- Evidence of management review or reporting to supervisory committee
- Sample testing results (penetration test report, vulnerability scan, backup recovery test)
- Risk-treatment documentation where a control is partially applied or excluded

---

## Common pitfalls

- Treating a policy document as equivalent to implementation evidence.
- Missing DKIM and DMARC when SPF was already deployed (2-4-3-5).
- SIEM that ingests logs but has no active use cases or alert rules — ingestion alone does not satisfy 2-12.
- Assuming data localisation no longer applies because 4-2-3-3 was deleted — it moved to NDMO, not disappeared.
- Third-party contracts without cybersecurity clauses, especially legacy agreements predating ECC-1.
- Offshore managed SOC assumed compliant because data does not leave KSA — the residency requirement (4-1-3-2) applies to the SOC centre, not the data.
- Non-Saudi contractors filling cybersecurity operational roles (1-2-2).
- Blanket MFA on web apps without an impact assessment — ECC-2 requires risk-based assessment of authentication factors (2-15-3-5).
- Risk assessments not triggered for the four mandatory scenarios in 1-5-3.

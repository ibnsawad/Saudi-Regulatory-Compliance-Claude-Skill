# NCA ECC-2:2024 — Essential Cybersecurity Controls

## At a glance

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Issuer | National Cybersecurity Authority (NCA), Kingdom of Saudi Arabia |
| Document | Essential Cybersecurity Controls (ECC-2:2024) |
| Supersedes | ECC-1:2018 |
| Status | Mandatory for in-scope entities |
| Native language | Arabic; English version is reference only |

## Applicability

Applies to:

- Government ministries, authorities, and public institutions
- State-owned and state-affiliated enterprises
- Operators of national critical infrastructure
- Private-sector entities designated by NCA or by sectoral regulators that adopt ECC

Does not by itself apply to entities supervised solely by SAMA (which use SAMA CSF) or those covered exclusively by sector-specific frameworks. However, SAMA-supervised entities often align with ECC where they operate non-financial critical systems.

## Structure

ECC-2:2024 retains a four-domain structure with a hierarchical numbering scheme: **Main Domain → Subdomain → Control → Subcontrol**, written as `M-S-C` or `M-S-C-SC`.

The four main domains:

1. **Cybersecurity Governance** — leadership, strategy, policy, roles, risk management, programme management, human-resources cybersecurity, awareness, audit and review.
2. **Cybersecurity Defence** — asset management, identity and access, system and information protection, email and web protection, networks, mobile devices, data protection, cryptography, BYOD, secure configuration, change management, vulnerability management, penetration testing, application security, event logging and monitoring, cybersecurity incident management, physical security.
3. **Cybersecurity Resilience** — business continuity aspects of cybersecurity (BCM integration, resilience testing, recovery).
4. **Third-Party and Cloud Computing Cybersecurity** — third-party cybersecurity, cloud computing cybersecurity (with explicit alignment to CCC).

A subset of controls is marked as **Subcontrols** that drill into specific implementation expectations. Many controls are written as outcomes ("the entity must ensure …") supported by mandatory implementation guidance.

## What changed from ECC-1:2018

Material differences a user should know about:

- Restructured from five to four main domains (the previous "Third-Party Cybersecurity" and "Cloud Cybersecurity" domains are merged).
- Strengthened cloud, resilience, and supply-chain expectations.
- More explicit cross-references to CCC, DCC, CSCC, and OTCC.
- Continuous monitoring and measurable assessment now expected, not optional.
- Tighter language around log retention, identity governance, and patch timelines.

When a user references ECC-1, treat it as legacy and flag the restructure in your output.

## High-frequency controls (audit hot spots)

These are the controls most often raised in NCA gap assessments. Use them as a triage list.

- Cybersecurity strategy and policy approved at board / top management
- Documented and tested cybersecurity risk-management methodology
- Asset inventory with classification and ownership
- Privileged access management with vaulting and session monitoring
- MFA on all remote and privileged access
- Centralised log collection (SIEM) with use-case coverage and 12-month retention as a baseline
- 24/7 monitoring or equivalent SOC arrangement
- Vulnerability management with defined remediation SLAs by severity
- Patch management with timelines tied to severity
- Penetration testing on internet-facing and critical systems at defined intervals
- Encryption of sensitive data at rest and in transit, with key management
- Documented incident-response plan with tested playbooks
- Secure configuration baselines and hardening standards
- Awareness programme with completion tracking
- Third-party cybersecurity due diligence and contractual clauses
- Cloud-specific governance referencing CCC

## Compliance levels

NCA expects entities to achieve and report compliance levels per a published assessment methodology. Common practice:

- **Level 1 — Not implemented**
- **Level 2 — Partially implemented**
- **Level 3 — Implemented**
- **Level 4 — Implemented and documented**
- **Level 5 — Implemented, documented, and effective (assessed)**

NCA's compliance tool ("Haseen" platform for some sectors) is used for self-assessment submission by certain entities — confirm with the user whether they are onboarded.

## Evidence expectations

For each control, an auditor will typically request:

- Approved policy or procedure
- Recent records of operation (tickets, logs, exception registers)
- Evidence of management review or board reporting
- Sample testing results
- Risk-treatment decisions where the control is partially applied

## Common pitfalls

- Treating policies as compliance — they're necessary but not sufficient.
- Missing third-party clauses in legacy contracts.
- SIEM that ingests but does not alert on, or correlate, control-relevant use cases.
- "Critical system" is not formally designated, so CSCC obligations are missed.
- Cloud responsibilities not split between CSP and tenant in line with CCC.

Domain headings above are accurate to ECC-2:2024 as published by NCA. Specific control numbers are not transcribed here because exact codes vary by subcontrol and any published number must be confirmed against the official NCA document. When citing a control, fetch it from the official ECC-2:2024 PDF or ask the user to provide it; do not invent numbering.

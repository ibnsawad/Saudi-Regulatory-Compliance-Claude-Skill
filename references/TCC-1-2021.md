## Purpose

The TCC establishes the minimum cybersecurity requirements to enable national organisations to perform telework in a secure manner. Issued because increasing dependence on telework increases cyber threats and risks to telework systems, requiring cybersecurity requirements to reduce these threats and risks alongside the ECC baseline.

**Stated objectives:**
1. Enable an organisation's work to be performed remotely in a secure manner and adapt to changes in the business environment and telework systems.
2. Enhance the organisation's cybersecurity capabilities and resilience where telework is exposed to cyber threats that could result in negative impacts and costly losses.
3. Contribute to raising the level of cybersecurity at the national level.

---

## Applicability

**Mandatory in-scope entities — every organisation that allows telework:**
- Government organisations in KSA: ministries, authorities, establishments, and similar bodies
- Companies and entities affiliated with government organisations
- Private-sector organisations owning, operating, or hosting Critical National Infrastructure (CNIs)

All in-scope entities are collectively referred to as "The Organisation" in the TCC.

**Voluntary:** NCA strongly encourages all other KSA organisations to leverage these controls as best practice.

**Technology-specific applicability:** Controls in subdomain 3-1 (Cloud Computing and Hosting Cybersecurity) apply only to organisations currently using or planning to use cloud computing and hosting services for telework.

**Compliance trigger:** Every organisation that **allows telework** must comply with all applicable controls. Compliance with the ECC is a prerequisite — TCC controls operate *in addition to* ECC, not as alternatives.

### 1-1 Cybersecurity Policies and Procedures

**Objective:** To ensure that cybersecurity requirements are documented, communicated and complied with by the organisation as per related laws and regulations, and organisational requirements.

**ECC reference:** This subdomain adds to ECC control 1-3-1.

| Control ID | Control Clause |
|------------|----------------|
| 1-1-1 | Referring to control 1-3-1 in the ECC, cybersecurity policies and procedures must include the following: |
| 1-1-1-1 | Defining and documenting the telework cybersecurity requirements and controls as part of the organisation's cybersecurity policies. |

**Audit flag — HIGH:** Most organisations have a general cybersecurity policy but do not specifically document telework controls within it. The TCC explicitly requires telework requirements to be incorporated into the cybersecurity policy, not maintained only in operational procedures. Absence of telework-specific policy language in the main cybersecurity policy document is a gap.

**Evidence an auditor will request:**

| Evidence | Control ref |
|----------|-------------|
| Cybersecurity policy document with a dedicated telework section or annex that references TCC requirements | 1-1-1-1 |
| Date of last policy review/approval showing telework content was reviewed | 1-1-1-1 |
| Evidence that the policy was communicated to all employees who telework (distribution records, acknowledgement signatures) | 1-1-1 |

### 1-2 Cybersecurity Risk Management

**Objective:** To ensure managing cybersecurity risks in a methodological approach in order to protect the organisation's information and technology assets as per organisational policies and procedures, and related laws and regulations.

**ECC reference:** This subdomain adds to ECC subdomain 1-5.

| Control ID | Control Clause |
|------------|----------------|
| 1-2-1 | In addition to the controls within subdomain 1-5 in the ECC, requirements for cybersecurity risk management should include at least the following: |
| 1-2-1-1 | Assessment of the cybersecurity risks for telework systems, **once per year at least**. |
| 1-2-1-2 | Assessment of cybersecurity risks during planning and **before permitting telework** for any service or system. |
| 1-2-1-3 | Including the cybersecurity risks related to telework systems and its related services and systems in the entity's cybersecurity **risk register**, and monitoring it at least once a year. |

**Audit flag — HIGH (1-2-1-2):** The requirement to assess risks *before permitting* telework for any new service or system is frequently overlooked. Organisations that spin up new telework capabilities (new VPN endpoints, new SaaS collaboration tools, expanded remote access) without a prior risk assessment are non-compliant. NCA auditors look for evidence that each telework enablement decision was preceded by a documented risk assessment.

**Audit flag — MEDIUM (1-2-1-3):** Telework-specific risks must appear in the main risk register and be reviewed at least annually. Maintaining telework risks only in a separate departmental log that is not part of the official risk register is a gap.

**Evidence an auditor will request:**

| Evidence | Control ref |
|----------|-------------|
| Dated telework risk assessment conducted within the last 12 months | 1-2-1-1 |
| Pre-telework enablement risk assessments for any services or systems approved for remote access in the last assessment period | 1-2-1-2 |
| Risk register extract showing telework system risks with dates of last review | 1-2-1-3 |
| Evidence of annual review of telework risks in the risk register (board/committee minutes, review sign-off) | 1-2-1-3 |

### 1-3 Cybersecurity Awareness and Training Program

**Objective:** To ensure that personnel are aware of their cybersecurity responsibilities and have the essential cybersecurity awareness. To ensure that personnel are provided with the required cybersecurity training, skills and credentials needed to accomplish their cybersecurity responsibilities and to protect the organisation's information and technology assets.

**ECC references:** This subdomain adds to ECC controls 1-10-3 and 1-10-4.

| Control ID | Control Clause |
|------------|----------------|
| 1-3-1 | In addition to the sub-controls within control 1-10-3 in the ECC, the cybersecurity awareness program must cover awareness about the potential cyber risks and threats related to telework, including the following: |
| 1-3-1-1 | Secure use of telework devices and how to protect them. |
| 1-3-1-2 | Secure handling of identities and passwords. |
| 1-3-1-3 | Protection of the stored data on the telework devices, and to be handled based on its classification. |
| 1-3-1-4 | Secure handling of applications and solutions used for telework such as: virtual conferencing and collaboration, and file sharing solutions. |
| 1-3-1-5 | Secure handling of home networks, making sure it is configured in a secure way. |
| 1-3-1-6 | Avoidance of teleworking using unreliable public devices or networks or while in public places. |
| 1-3-1-7 | Unauthorized physical access, loss, theft, and sabotage of technical assets and telework systems. |
| 1-3-1-8 | To communicate directly with the cybersecurity department if a cybersecurity threat is suspected. |
| 1-3-2 | In addition to the sub-controls within control 1-10-4 in the ECC, employees must be trained with the required technical skills to ensure the implementation of the cybersecurity requirements when handling telework systems. |

**Audit flag — HIGH (1-3-1):** Standard annual cybersecurity awareness programs rarely include telework-specific topics (home network security, BYOD handling, VPN use, public Wi-Fi risks). Auditors will examine awareness content to verify all eight listed topics are covered. A generic phishing awareness program does not satisfy this requirement.

**Audit flag — MEDIUM (1-3-2):** Technical staff who administer telework systems (VPN, MDM, remote access gateways) require role-specific technical training on telework system cybersecurity requirements — not just general awareness. This is separate from the 1-3-1 employee awareness requirement.

**Evidence an auditor will request:**

| Evidence | Control ref |
|----------|-------------|
| Cybersecurity awareness program curriculum/agenda showing all 8 TCC telework topics are addressed | 1-3-1-1 through 1-3-1-8 |
| Training completion records for all teleworking employees | 1-3-1 |
| Separate technical training records for IT/cybersecurity staff managing telework systems | 1-3-2 |
| Training materials covering secure home network configuration, BYOD handling, VPN use, public network avoidance | 1-3-1-5 and 1-3-1-6 |
| Evidence of awareness program update dates — confirming telework content was reviewed/refreshed | 1-3-1 |

### 2-1 Asset Management

**Objective:** To ensure that the organisation has an accurate and detailed inventory of information and technology assets in order to support the organisation's cybersecurity and operational requirements to maintain the confidentiality, integrity and availability of information and technology assets.

**ECC reference:** This subdomain adds to ECC subdomain 2-1.

| Control ID | Control Clause |
|------------|----------------|
| 2-1-1 | In addition to the controls within subdomain 2-1 in the ECC, cybersecurity requirements for asset management related to telework systems should include at least the following: |
| 2-1-1-1 | Identifying and maintaining an **annually-updated** inventory of information and technology assets of the telework systems. |

**Audit flag — MEDIUM:** Organisations often maintain a general IT asset inventory but do not separately identify telework-specific assets (remote access devices, VPN gateways, telework endpoints, MDM-enrolled devices, collaboration platforms). NCA auditors will check that the asset inventory specifically covers telework system components and that it shows evidence of annual update.

**Evidence an auditor will request:**

| Evidence | Control ref |
|----------|-------------|
| Asset inventory extract specifically listing telework system assets (VPN gateways, remote desktops, MDM-enrolled endpoints, collaboration tools) | 2-1-1-1 |
| Date-stamp or version history showing the inventory was updated within the last 12 months | 2-1-1-1 |

### 2-2 Identity and Access Management

**Objective:** To ensure the secure and restricted logical access to information and technology assets in order to prevent unauthorized access and allow only authorized access for users which are necessary to accomplish assigned tasks.

**ECC reference:** This subdomain adds to ECC control 2-2-3 and subcontrol 2-2-3-5.

| Control ID | Control Clause |
|------------|----------------|
| 2-2-1 | In addition to the sub-controls within control 2-2-3 in the ECC, cybersecurity requirements for identity and access management related to telework systems shall include at least the following: |
| 2-2-1-1 | Managing telework access rights based on need, considering the sensitivity of the systems, the level of access rights and the type of devices used by employees for telework. |
| 2-2-1-2 | Restricting remote access for the same user from multiple computers at the same time (Concurrent Logins). |
| 2-2-1-3 | Using secure standards to manage identities and passwords used in the telework systems. |
| 2-2-2 | With reference to ECC subcontrol 2-2-3-5, user's identities and access rights used for telework must be reviewed **at least once every year**. |

**Audit flag — CRITICAL (2-2-1-2):** Concurrent login restrictions are specifically called out because credential theft and sharing in remote-work environments creates parallel active sessions. Many organisations do not implement session concurrency controls on VPN or remote access platforms. NCA auditors will test whether the configuration prevents simultaneous logins from multiple sources for the same user account.

**Audit flag — HIGH (2-2-1-1):** Need-based access for telework should reflect both the sensitivity of systems being accessed and the type of device (corporate-managed vs. BYOD). Granting full internal network access to BYOD devices without additional controls is a common gap.

**Audit flag — MEDIUM (2-2-2):** Annual access rights review must cover telework-specific accounts and permissions — not just general system access. If the general IAM review does not explicitly address remote access privileges and VPN credentials, it does not satisfy this control.

**Evidence an auditor will request:**

| Evidence | Control ref |
|----------|-------------|
| Access control policy section addressing telework access (need-based, device-sensitivity-aware) | 2-2-1-1 |
| VPN/remote access system configuration screenshots or documentation showing concurrent login restrictions are enabled | 2-2-1-2 |
| Password/identity management standards document showing approved standards for telework systems | 2-2-1-3 |
| Annual user access rights review records specifically covering telework/remote access accounts | 2-2-2 |
| Evidence of de-provisioning for users no longer requiring telework access | 2-2-2 |

### 2-3 Information System and Processing Facilities Protection

**Objective:** To ensure the protection of information systems and information processing facilities (including workstations and infrastructures) against cyber risks.

**ECC reference:** This subdomain adds to ECC control 2-3-3.

| Control ID | Control Clause |
|------------|----------------|
| 2-3-1 | In addition to the subcontrols in ECC control 2-3-3, cybersecurity requirements for protecting telework systems and information processing facilities must include at least the following: |
| 2-3-1-1 | Applying updates and security patches for telework systems **at least once every three months**. |
| 2-3-1-2 | Reviewing telework systems' configurations and hardening **at least once every year**. |
| 2-3-1-3 | Reviewing and changing default configurations, and ensuring the **removal of hard-coded, backdoor and/or default passwords**. |
| 2-3-1-4 | Securing Session Management which includes the session authenticity, lockout, and timeout. |
| 2-3-1-5 | Restricting the activation of the features and services of the telework systems based on needs, provided that potential cyber risks are analyzed in case there is a need to activate them. |

**Audit flag — CRITICAL (2-3-1-3):** Default passwords and hard-coded credentials in remote access systems (VPN concentrators, remote desktop gateways, collaboration platforms) are among the most commonly exploited entry points. NCA treats unchanged default credentials on any telework system component as an immediate high-severity finding.

**Audit flag — HIGH (2-3-1-1):** The explicit three-month patch cycle for telework systems is more frequent than the general annual or biannual patching cycles many organisations maintain. Remote-access systems are high-priority targets; patching frequency must be documented and verifiable through patch logs.

**Audit flag — HIGH (2-3-1-4):** Session management controls (session timeout, automatic lockout after inactivity, session authenticity verification) are frequently missing on legacy VPN or remote desktop configurations. Auditors will check session timeout settings and lockout policies on all remote access components.

**Evidence an auditor will request:**

| Evidence | Control ref |
|----------|-------------|
| Patch management logs for telework systems showing patching dates within the required 3-month cycle | 2-3-1-1 |
| Annual hardening review report for telework systems (with dated evidence) | 2-3-1-2 |
| Configuration review records showing default password and credential audits, with evidence of changes made | 2-3-1-3 |
| Session management configuration documentation (timeout values, lockout thresholds) for VPN/remote desktop/collaboration systems | 2-3-1-4 |
| Feature/service inventory for telework systems showing only necessary services are enabled, with risk analysis for any non-standard activations | 2-3-1-5 |

### 2-4 Network Security Management

**Objective:** To ensure the protection of organisation's network from cyber risks.

**ECC reference:** This subdomain adds to ECC control 2-5-3.

| Control ID | Control Clause |
|------------|----------------|
| 2-4-1 | In addition to the subcontrols in ECC control 2-5-3, cybersecurity requirements of telework systems' network security management must include at least the following: |
| 2-4-1-1 | Restrictions on network services, protocols and ports used to access remotely, specifically to internal systems and to only be opened based on need. |
| 2-4-1-2 | Reviewing firewall rules and configurations, **at least once every year**. |
| 2-4-1-3 | Protecting against Distributed Denial of Service Attack (DDoS) attacks to limit risks arising from these attacks. |
| 2-4-1-4 | Protecting against Advanced Persistent Threats (APT) at the network layer. |

**Audit flag — HIGH (2-4-1-1):** Wide-open remote access configurations where all internal ports and services are accessible over VPN are a common gap. The TCC requires that only necessary ports and protocols are opened — a least-privilege network access model.

**Audit flag — HIGH (2-4-1-2):** Annual firewall rule review specifically for remote-access rules and configurations is required. Organisations that review firewall rules generally but do not specifically review remote-access-related rules against need may not satisfy this control.

**Audit flag — MEDIUM (2-4-1-4):** APT protection at the network layer (advanced threat detection, next-generation firewalls, network traffic analysis) is required for telework network paths — not just endpoint protection.

**Evidence an auditor will request:**

| Evidence | Control ref |
|----------|-------------|
| Network diagram showing remote access architecture with port/protocol restrictions documented | 2-4-1-1 |
| Firewall rule review records with dates showing annual review cadence | 2-4-1-2 |
| Evidence of DDoS protection on remote access infrastructure (ISP-level, CDN-level, or dedicated appliance) | 2-4-1-3 |
| Documentation of APT protection mechanisms at the network layer (NGFW, IDS/IPS, network traffic analysis tool) covering telework traffic paths | 2-4-1-4 |

### 2-5 Mobile Device Security

**Objective:** To ensure the protection of mobile devices (including laptops, smartphones, tablets) from cyber risks and to ensure the secure handling of the organisation's information (including sensitive information) while utilizing Bring Your Own Device (BYOD) policy.

**ECC reference:** This subdomain adds to ECC control 2-6-3.

| Control ID | Control Clause |
|------------|----------------|
| 2-5-1 | In addition to the sub-controls within control 2-6-3 in the ECC, cybersecurity requirements for mobile device security related to telework systems shall include at least the following: |
| 2-5-1-1 | Central management of mobile devices and BYODs using a **Mobile Device Management system (MDM)**. |
| 2-5-1-2 | Applying updates and security patches on mobile devices, **at least once every month**. |

**Audit flag — CRITICAL (2-5-1-1):** MDM for both corporate-owned and BYOD devices used for telework is explicitly mandated. Organisations that permit BYOD telework without MDM enrollment are non-compliant. NCA auditors will check MDM enrollment coverage versus the population of teleworking users.

**Audit flag — HIGH (2-5-1-2):** Monthly patching of mobile devices is more frequent than the three-month cycle for telework systems generally. Mobile devices are high-risk endpoints in telework scenarios. Patch compliance dashboards from the MDM system will be requested.

**Evidence an auditor will request:**

| Evidence | Control ref |
|----------|-------------|
| MDM solution name and evidence that all teleworking devices (corporate and BYOD) are enrolled | 2-5-1-1 |
| MDM enrollment coverage report showing percentage of teleworking users with enrolled devices | 2-5-1-1 |
| MDM patch compliance report showing devices patched within the last 30 days and devices overdue | 2-5-1-2 |
| BYOD policy document confirming MDM enrollment is a condition of BYOD telework access | 2-5-1-1 |

### 2-6 Data and Information Protection

**Objective:** To ensure the confidentiality, integrity and availability of organisation's data and information as per organisational policies and procedures, and related laws and regulations.

**ECC reference:** This subdomain adds to ECC control 2-7-3.

| Control ID | Control Clause |
|------------|----------------|
| 2-6-1 | In addition to the subcontrols in ECC control 2-7-3, cybersecurity requirements for protecting and handling data and information must include at least the following: |
| 2-6-1-1 | Identifying classified data, according to the relevant regulations, that can be used, accessed or dealt with through telework systems. |
| 2-6-1-2 | Protecting classified data which was identified in control 2-6-1-1, using controls such as: not allowing the use of a specific type of classified data, or by the use of technology (e.g. Data Leakage Prevention); such controls and technologies can be determined by analyzing the cyber risks of the organisation. |

**Audit flag — HIGH (2-6-1-1):** The organisation must have a documented inventory or classification of data types that teleworkers are permitted to access, handle, or store locally. Many organisations allow broad data access over VPN without a deliberate classification of what telework-accessible data exists and at what classification level.

**Audit flag — HIGH (2-6-1-2):** The control requires an explicit decision — either prohibiting access to certain classified data through telework systems, or applying technical controls (DLP) to protect it. Allowing all classified data to flow through VPN without either a prohibition or a DLP control is non-compliant.

**Evidence an auditor will request:**

| Evidence | Control ref |
|----------|-------------|
| Data classification register or inventory identifying which classification levels of data are accessible through telework systems | 2-6-1-1 |
| Risk analysis documenting the decision on how each classification level is treated in telework contexts | 2-6-1-2 |
| DLP configuration evidence showing rules protecting classified data accessed through telework, OR documented prohibition of specific classified data from telework access | 2-6-1-2 |

### 2-7 Cryptography

**Objective:** To ensure that cryptography requirements are applied to protect telework communications in accordance with national standards.

**ECC reference:** This subdomain adds to ECC control 2-8-3.

| Control ID | Control Clause |
|------------|----------------|
| 2-7-1 | In addition to the sub-controls within control 2-8-3 in the ECC, cybersecurity requirements for cryptography related to telework systems shall include at least the following: |
| 2-7-1-1 | The use of updated and secure methods and algorithms for **encryption over the entire network connection used for telework**, according to the **Advanced level within the National Cryptography Standards (NCS 1:2020)**. |

**Audit flag — CRITICAL (2-7-1-1):** This control requires full-tunnel encryption of all telework network connections at the NCS 1:2020 Advanced level. This is a specific KSA national standard requirement — not simply "use TLS." Organisations must verify their VPN and remote access encryption configurations meet the NCS 1:2020 Advanced-level algorithm and key-length requirements. Split tunnelling that leaves parts of the connection unencrypted may not satisfy this control. Deprecated algorithms (RC4, DES, 3DES, MD5) are explicitly excluded.

**Evidence an auditor will request:**

| Evidence | Control ref |
|----------|-------------|
| VPN and remote access configuration documentation showing encryption algorithms, key lengths, and cipher suites in use | 2-7-1-1 |
| Cryptographic standards policy showing alignment with NCS 1:2020 Advanced level | 2-7-1-1 |
| Evidence that weak/deprecated algorithms are disabled on all telework connection endpoints | 2-7-1-1 |
| NCS 1:2020 compliance attestation or review record | 2-7-1-1 |

### 2-8 Backup and Recovery Management

**Objective:** To ensure the protection of organisation's data and information including information systems and software configurations from cyber risks as per organisational policies and procedures, and related laws and regulations.

**ECC reference:** This subdomain adds to ECC control 2-9-3 and subcontrol 2-9-3-3.

| Control ID | Control Clause |
|------------|----------------|
| 2-8-1 | In addition to the subcontrols in ECC control 2-9-3, cybersecurity requirements for backup and recovery management must include at least the following: |
| 2-8-1-1 | Performing backup within planned intervals, **according to the organisation's risk assessment**. |
| 2-8-2 | With reference to ECC subcontrol 2-9-3-3, a periodical test must be conducted **at least once every six months** in order to determine the efficiency of recovering telework systems backups. |

**Audit flag — HIGH (2-8-2):** The explicit six-month recovery test requirement for telework systems is more frequent than many organisations' general annual backup testing cycle. NCA auditors will request two dated backup recovery test reports showing telework systems were tested within a 12-month period.

**Audit flag — MEDIUM (2-8-1-1):** Backup intervals for telework systems must be documented and justified by the risk assessment. An organisation with no documented link between its risk assessment findings and its backup schedule for telework systems has a gap.

**Evidence an auditor will request:**

| Evidence | Control ref |
|----------|-------------|
| Backup policy/schedule for telework systems with documented link to risk assessment | 2-8-1-1 |
| Backup logs for telework systems showing backups completed at the planned intervals | 2-8-1-1 |
| Two dated backup recovery test reports for telework systems within the last 12 months (bi-annual requirement) | 2-8-2 |
| Recovery test results showing RTOs/RPOs were met | 2-8-2 |

### 2-9 Vulnerabilities Management

**Objective:** To ensure timely detection and effective remediation of technical vulnerabilities to prevent or minimize the probability of exploiting these vulnerabilities to launch cyber attacks against the organisation.

**ECC reference:** This subdomain adds to ECC control 2-10-3.

| Control ID | Control Clause |
|------------|----------------|
| 2-9-1 | In addition to the subcontrols in ECC control 2-10-3, cybersecurity requirements for technical vulnerabilities management of telework systems must include at least the following: |
| 2-9-1-1 | Assessing vulnerabilities on technical components of telework systems, and to be classified based on criticality, **at least once every three months**. |
| 2-9-1-2 | Remediating vulnerabilities for telework systems, **at least once every three months**. |

**Audit flag — HIGH (2-9-1-1 and 2-9-1-2):** Both vulnerability assessment and remediation are required on a three-month cycle for telework systems — more frequently than many organisations' general vulnerability scanning schedules. Remote access infrastructure (VPN, RDP, reverse proxies) is a priority attack surface. Auditors will request scan reports with coverage of all telework system components and remediation evidence within the three-month window.

**Evidence an auditor will request:**

| Evidence | Control ref |
|----------|-------------|
| Vulnerability scan reports covering all telework system technical components, dated within the last 3 months | 2-9-1-1 |
| Vulnerability classification report showing criticality assignments | 2-9-1-1 |
| Remediation records/ticket closure evidence showing vulnerabilities in telework systems addressed within 3 months | 2-9-1-2 |
| Explanation/risk-acceptance records for any vulnerabilities not remediated within the required cycle | 2-9-1-2 |

### 2-10 Penetration Testing

**Objective:** To assess and evaluate the efficiency of the organisation's cybersecurity defense capabilities through simulated cyber-attacks to discover unknown weaknesses within the technical infrastructure that may lead to a cyber breach.

**ECC reference:** This subdomain adds to ECC control 2-11-3 and subcontrol 2-11-3-2.

| Control ID | Control Clause |
|------------|----------------|
| 2-10-1 | In addition to the sub-controls within control 2-11-3 in the ECC, cybersecurity requirements for penetration testing related to telework systems shall include at least the following: |
| 2-10-1-1 | **Scope of penetration tests must cover all of the telework systems' technical components**. |
| 2-10-2 | With reference to ECC subcontrol 2-11-3-2, penetration tests must be conducted on telework systems **at least once every year**. |

**Audit flag — HIGH (2-10-1-1):** Organisations that conduct annual penetration tests but exclude telework infrastructure (VPN gateways, remote desktop services, MDM servers, collaboration platforms) from scope are non-compliant. All telework system components must be in the pentest scope.

**Audit flag — HIGH (2-10-2):** Annual penetration testing of telework systems is the minimum requirement. The pentest report must explicitly identify telework systems in its scope statement.

**Evidence an auditor will request:**

| Evidence | Control ref |
|----------|-------------|
| Most recent penetration test report (within last 12 months) with scope statement explicitly listing telework system components | 2-10-1-1 |
| Evidence that all telework technical components were included in scope (VPN, remote desktop, MDM, collaboration tools) | 2-10-1-1 |
| Remediation plan and evidence of findings addressed from the pentest | 2-10-2 |
| Pentest engagement confirmation letter or SOW showing annual frequency | 2-10-2 |

### 2-11 Cybersecurity Event Logs and Monitoring Management

**Objective:** To ensure timely collection, analysis and monitoring of cybersecurity events for early detection of potential cyber-attacks in order to prevent or minimize the negative impacts on the organisation's operations.

**ECC references:** This subdomain adds to ECC control 2-12-3 and subcontrol 2-12-3-5.

| Control ID | Control Clause |
|------------|----------------|
| 2-11-1 | In addition to the subcontrols in ECC control 2-12-3, cybersecurity requirements for event logs and monitoring management for telework systems must include at least the following: |
| 2-11-1-1 | Activating cybersecurity events logs on **all technical components** of telework systems. |
| 2-11-1-2 | Monitoring and analyzing **user behavior (UBA)**. |
| 2-11-1-3 | Monitoring telework systems events **around the clock (24/7)**. |
| 2-11-1-4 | Updating and implementing cybersecurity monitoring procedures around the clock, to include **monitoring remote access operations, especially remote access from outside the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia**, after checking their authenticity. |
| 2-11-2 | With reference to ECC subcontrol 2-12-3-5, retention period of cybersecurity's telework systems event logs must be **12 months minimum**, in accordance with relevant legislative and regulatory requirements. |

**Audit flag — CRITICAL (2-11-1-3 and 2-11-1-4):** 24/7 monitoring of telework systems is explicitly required. Organisations that rely on business-hours-only SOC coverage for telework monitoring are non-compliant. Remote access from outside KSA must be specifically flagged and authenticated — this is a geographic anomaly detection requirement.

**Audit flag — HIGH (2-11-1-2):** User Behavior Analytics (UBA) is explicitly required — not just event log collection. UBA requires analysis tools capable of establishing behavioural baselines and detecting deviations. SIEM log collection without a UBA/UEBA capability does not fully satisfy this control.

**Audit flag — HIGH (2-11-1-1):** All telework system components must have logging enabled. Gaps in log coverage (collaboration tools not feeding into SIEM, VPN concentrators with partial logging, MDM servers not logging policy violations) are audit findings.

**Audit flag — HIGH (2-11-2):** The 12-month log retention requirement applies specifically to telework system event logs. Organisations with shorter retention periods (30 or 90 days) for telework logs have a specific compliance gap.

**Evidence an auditor will request:**

| Evidence | Control ref |
|----------|-------------|
| Log source inventory showing all telework system components are feeding into SIEM/log management | 2-11-1-1 |
| UBA/UEBA tool configuration or deployment evidence | 2-11-1-2 |
| SOC or monitoring SLA/schedule showing 24/7 coverage of telework systems | 2-11-1-3 |
| Monitoring procedure document showing remote access monitoring is included, with specific mention of out-of-KSA access alerting | 2-11-1-4 |
| Log retention configuration showing 12-month minimum for telework system logs | 2-11-2 |
| Sample alert records for remote access anomalies detected and investigated | 2-11-1-4 |

### 3-1 Cloud Computing and Hosting Cybersecurity

**Objective:** To ensure the proper and efficient remediation of cyber risks and the implementation of cybersecurity requirements related to hosting and cloud computing as per organisational policies and procedures, and related laws and regulations. **Applicability:** Controls in subdomain 3-1 apply only to organisations currently using or planning to use cloud computing and hosting services for telework.

**ECC reference:** This subdomain adds to ECC control 4-2-3.

**Audit flag — CRITICAL (3-1-1-1):** Data residency for telework systems hosted on cloud or third-party infrastructure must be within KSA. ## Audit hot spots — triage list

Ranked by criticality in NCA compliance assessments:

1. **No MDM for BYOD telework devices (2-5-1-1) — CRITICAL:** MDM enrollment is explicitly required for all mobile devices including BYOD used for telework. This is one of the most commonly failed controls.

2. **Concurrent login not restricted (2-2-1-2) — CRITICAL:** Remote access systems configured to allow the same user to log in from multiple devices simultaneously. Auditors test this technically on VPN and remote desktop systems.

3. **Default/hard-coded passwords on telework infrastructure (2-3-1-3) — CRITICAL:** VPN concentrators, remote access gateways, MDM servers with unchanged default credentials. NCA treats this as an immediate critical finding.

4. **Telework encryption not at NCS 1:2020 Advanced level (2-7-1-1) — CRITICAL:** VPN and remote access connections using deprecated cipher suites or below NCS Advanced-level specification. Organisations that have not explicitly mapped their VPN cipher configuration to NCS 1:2020 requirements have a gap.

5. **Cloud-hosted telework systems outside KSA (3-1-1-1) — CRITICAL:** VDI, VPN-as-a-Service, or SaaS collaboration platforms hosted in non-KSA cloud regions. Auditors verify provider datacenter locations.

6. **No 24/7 monitoring of telework events (2-11-1-3 and 2-11-1-4) — CRITICAL:** Business-hours-only monitoring of telework systems. Out-of-KSA remote access alerts require continuous monitoring.

7. **UBA not deployed (2-11-1-2) — HIGH:** Organisations with SIEM collecting logs but no User Behavior Analytics capability.

8. **Quarterly patching cycle not achieved for telework systems (2-3-1-1) — HIGH:** Annual or biannual patching of telework infrastructure rather than quarterly.

9. **Pentest scope excludes telework components (2-10-1-1) — HIGH:** Annual pentests that test the corporate network perimeter but exclude VPN gateways, MDM servers, and remote desktop systems.

10. **Telework awareness program lacks specific TCC topics (1-3-1) — HIGH:** Generic phishing awareness training presented as TCC compliance. Auditors check content against all eight topics in 1-3-1-1 through 1-3-1-8.

11. **No pre-enablement risk assessment for new telework services (1-2-1-2) — HIGH:** Adding new collaboration tools or expanding VPN access without a prior risk assessment.

12. **Six-month backup recovery tests not performed (2-8-2) — HIGH:** Recovery testing conducted annually rather than every six months for telework systems.

13. **Quarterly vulnerability scans not covering all telework components (2-9-1-1) — HIGH:** Scans that omit collaboration platforms, MDM servers, or cloud-hosted telework infrastructure.

14. **Incident response plan not updated for telework (2-12-1-1) — HIGH:** IR plans assuming on-premise staff availability, without remote IR activation procedures.

15. **NCA/sector regulator alerts not tracked and implemented (2-12-1-3) — MEDIUM:** No documented process for receiving, reviewing, and implementing NCA advisories on telework threats.

## Key definitions (TCC Appendix B)

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Telework Systems | Any technical systems, means or tools and related components used by the organisation to enable employees to perform job duties in a place other than the official workplace. Examples include: virtual meeting systems, collaboration systems, file sharing, virtual private network (VPN), remote access systems, and other systems used in the work environment. |
| User Behavior Analytics (UBA) | The process of tracking and collecting user data, analyzing it, and defining patterns of user activity to detect harmful or unusual behaviors. |
| Data Leakage Prevention (DLP) | Technologies used to protect sensitive data from unauthorized disclosure, and to prevent its circulation outside the organization in any form; whether stored on volumes (at-rest), on user devices or servers (in-use), or in movement via the network (in-transit). |
| Distributed Denial of Service Attack (DDoS) | An attempt to disable the system and make its services unavailable by sending many requests from more than one source at the same time. |
| Mobile Device Management (MDM) System | A technical system used to manage, monitor, and protect mobile devices by applying cybersecurity policies. |

## Common implementation pitfalls

- **Treating the ECC as sufficient for telework:** ECC compliance is a prerequisite but does not satisfy TCC requirements. TCC adds specific frequency requirements (quarterly patching, monthly mobile patching, semi-annual backup testing, 24/7 monitoring) that go beyond the ECC baseline.
- **Scoping telework only as VPN access:** Telework systems include collaboration platforms (Teams, Zoom, file sharing), cloud-hosted virtual desktops, MDM infrastructure, and remote support tools — not just VPN. All components must be covered by TCC controls.
- **BYOD without MDM as an accepted workaround:** There is no BYOD-without-MDM workaround in the TCC. MDM is explicitly required for all devices used for telework.
- **Assuming cloud provider compliance satisfies 3-1-1-1:** The organisation must verify and document that cloud-hosted telework systems physically reside within KSA. Contractual assurance alone without verification of the specific datacenter region is insufficient.
- **Concurrent login restriction overlooked in VPN configuration:** This is a specific technical control that many default VPN configurations do not enable. It must be explicitly configured and verified.
- **NCS 1:2020 Advanced level not verified:** Encryption "best practice" (e.g. TLS 1.2, AES-128) may not meet the NCS 1:2020 Advanced-level specification. The specific algorithm, key length, and cipher suite requirements of NCS 1:2020 Advanced level must be verified against the VPN/remote access configuration.
- **Pentest frequency vs. scope confusion:** Even if annual pentest frequency satisfies 2-10-2, if the scope excludes telework components it fails 2-10-1-1. Both frequency and scope requirements must be met simultaneously.

# NCA OTCC-1:2022 — Operational Technology Cybersecurity Controls

## At a glance

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Issuer | National Cybersecurity Authority (NCA), KSA |
| Document | Operational Technology Cybersecurity Controls (OTCC-1:2022) |
| Status | Mandatory for entities with in-scope OT environments |
| Relationship to ECC | OTCC overlays ECC for OT environments |
| Common alignment | IEC 62443 series, NIST SP 800-82 Rev 3 |

## Applicability

Applies where the entity operates Operational Technology, including:

- Industrial Control Systems (ICS), DCS, SCADA, PLCs, RTUs
- Building management and physical security systems where networked
- Process safety systems (with care — safety integrity is paramount)
- Energy generation, transmission, and distribution systems
- Oil and gas upstream / midstream / downstream control systems
- Water and wastewater control systems
- Manufacturing and industrial automation
- Transport (rail signalling, aviation operations) where designated

## Domain structure

OTCC follows ECC's main-domain structure adapted to OT realities:

1. **OT Cybersecurity Governance** — OT-specific strategy, policy, roles (often distinct from IT CISO scope), OT risk management with safety considerations.
2. **OT Cybersecurity Defence** — IT/OT segmentation (Purdue model, DMZ, conduits and zones), asset inventory of OT, secure remote access for vendors, secure configuration of HMIs and engineering workstations, removable-media controls, malware protection appropriate to OT, vulnerability management with safety review, patch management with planned outage windows, change control, identity and access for OT, jump hosts, monitoring of OT networks (passive sensing where active scanning is unsafe), incident management with safety integration.
3. **OT Cybersecurity Resilience** — resilient architecture, failover, ICS recovery, configuration backup of controllers, golden images.
4. **Third-Party OT Cybersecurity** — vendor remote access, OEM and integrator security, supply-chain assurance for OT components.

## OT-specific requirements that catch IT teams off guard

- **No active scanning** on production OT networks without coordination and a controlled approach. Many OT controllers are fragile.
- **Patching has long planning lead times** — driven by safety, certification, and outage windows; SLAs differ from IT.
- **Antivirus / EDR may not be supportable** on legacy controllers — compensating controls (whitelisting, network segmentation, monitoring) are required.
- **Changes are tied to safety case** — cybersecurity changes can require safety re-validation.
- **Removable media is a primary vector** — strict policy and scanning kiosks expected.
- **Vendor remote access** is high-risk and tightly controlled — jump hosts, recorded sessions, MFA, scheduled access only.
- **Backup of configurations**, not just data — controller logic, HMI projects, recipe files, certificates.

## Network architecture expectations

Entities are expected to implement zone-and-conduit segmentation aligned to the Purdue Reference Model:

- Level 4–5 (Enterprise / DMZ to internet)
- Level 3.5 (IDMZ between IT and OT)
- Level 3 (Operations management — historians, AV/patch servers)
- Level 2 (Supervisory — HMI, SCADA)
- Level 1 (Basic control — PLC, RTU, DCS controllers)
- Level 0 (Process — sensors, actuators)

Conduits between zones must be controlled, documented, and minimal.

## Audit hot spots

- IT and OT environments connected without an industrial DMZ
- No accurate OT asset inventory
- Engineering workstations with internet access
- Vendor remote access via shared accounts and VPN
- Antivirus signatures stale on OT-side servers
- No passive monitoring (Claroty / Nozomi / Dragos / equivalent) on OT networks
- Patch management documented for IT but absent for OT
- Backups of controllers not tested for restore

## Required artefacts

- OT-specific policy and procedures
- Zone and conduit diagram
- OT asset inventory with criticality and patch status
- Vendor access procedure and access logs
- Configuration backup register and restore-test evidence
- OT-specific incident-response runbooks integrating safety
- Patch and change records aligned to outage windows
- Monitoring deployment evidence and tuning records

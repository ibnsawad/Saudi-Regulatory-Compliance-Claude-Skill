## At a glance

## Purpose

Provides the minimum cybersecurity requirements for **critical systems** in all organisations, in addition to the Essential Cybersecurity Controls. Developed from international standards and best practices, it aims to build protection and cyber resilience capabilities and raise preparedness against growing threats that could cause significant national-level impacts.

## Critical Systems — definition and scoping

### Definition

Any system or network whose failure, unauthorised change to its operation, unauthorised access to it, or to the data stored or processed by it; may result in negative impact on the organisation's businesses and services' availability, or cause negative economic, financial, security or social impacts on the national level.

### Identification criteria

An organisation must apply these seven criteria to identify which of its systems qualify as critical:

| # | Criterion |
|---|-----------|
| 1 | Negative impact on national security |
| 2 | Negative impact on the Kingdom's reputation and public image |
| 3 | Significant financial losses (more than 0.01% of GDP) |
| 4 | Negative impact on services provided to more than 5% of the population |
| 5 | Loss of lives |
| 6 | Unauthorised disclosure of data classified as Top Secret or Secret |
| 7 | Negative impact on the operations of one or more vital sectors |

**Practical note:** Organisations must formally document which systems meet these criteria and maintain that designation register. NCA may challenge the scope; under-scoping (failing to designate a qualifying system) is a compliance breach.

### Components in scope

CSCC applies to all components of a designated critical system, including:

- Network devices (routers, switches, gateways), firewalls, IDS/IPS, APT protection devices
- Databases, storage assets, middleware, servers and operating systems, applications
- Encryption devices; peripherals (printers, scanners)
- Individuals in critical system supporting roles (users, privileged technical staff, operators, service providers)
- Documentation related to all of the above

---

## Applicability

Applies to organisations that own or operate critical systems, whether they are:

- Government organisations in KSA or abroad (ministries, authorities, establishments, embassies)
- Subsidiaries of government or private organisations

Subdomain **4-2** (Cloud Computing and Hosting Cybersecurity) is binding only on organisations currently using or planning to use cloud/hosting services for critical systems.

### Implementation steps required

1. Identify the organisation's critical systems using the identification criteria above.
2. Implement all necessary measures to comply within the compliance period defined by NCA, with cybersecurity risks assessed and managed during that period.
3. Ensure continuous compliance after the defined compliance period.

### 1-1 Cybersecurity Strategy

Extends ECC subdomain 1-1.

| Control | Obligation |
|---------|-----------|
| 1-1-1 | The organisation's cybersecurity strategy must **explicitly prioritise** the protection of its critical systems (in addition to all ECC strategy requirements) |

---

### 1-2 Cybersecurity Risk Management

Extends ECC subdomain 1-5.

| Control | Obligation |
|---------|-----------|
| 1-2-1-1 | Conduct a cybersecurity risk assessment on critical systems **at least once annually** |
| 1-2-1-2 | Create a cybersecurity risk register for critical systems and **review it at least once every month** |

**Audit hot spot:** Monthly risk register review (1-2-1-2) is significantly more frequent than the standard annual review under ECC — many organisations apply the ECC cadence and miss the CSCC requirement.

---

### 1-3 Cybersecurity in IT Project Management

Two main controls, each extending a different ECC control:

**1-3-1** (extends ECC 1-6-2) — Project management and change management for critical systems must additionally include:

| Sub-control | Obligation |
|-------------|-----------|
| 1-3-1-1 | Conduct a **stress test** to ensure the capacity of the various components |
| 1-3-1-2 | Ensure the implementation of **business continuity requirements** |

**1-3-2** (extends ECC 1-6-3) — Software and application development for critical systems must additionally include:

| Sub-control | Obligation |
|-------------|-----------|
| 1-3-2-1 | Conduct a **security source code review** before critical system release |
| 1-3-2-2 | Secure the **access, storage, documentation, and releases** of source code |
| 1-3-2-3 | Secure the authenticated **Application Programming Interface (API)** |
| 1-3-2-4 | Ensure **secure and trusted migration** of applications from testing to production, with deletion of all test data, IDs, and passwords before migration |

---

### 1-4 Periodical Cybersecurity Review and Audit

References ECC controls 1-8-1 and 1-8-2.

| Control | Obligation | Frequency |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| 1-4-1 | Internal cybersecurity function review of CSCC implementation | **At least annually** |
| 1-4-2 | Review by **independent parties within the organisation, outside the cybersecurity function** | **At least every three years** |

---

### 1-5 Cybersecurity in Human Resources

Extends ECC control 1-9-3 (pre-employment requirements).

| Sub-control | Obligation |
|-------------|-----------|
| 1-5-1-1 | **Screen or vet** candidates prior to employment in roles working on critical systems |
| 1-5-1-2 | Technical support and development positions for critical systems **must be filled with experienced Saudi professionals** |

**Critical compliance note:** 1-5-1-2 is a Saudization requirement specific to critical systems technical roles — distinct from, and in addition to, the ECC-2:2024 §1-2-2 blanket Saudization of all cybersecurity positions. Both obligations run concurrently for entities subject to both ECC-2 and CSCC.

| Sub-control | Obligation |
|-------------|-----------|
| 2-1-1-1 | Maintain an **annually-updated inventory** of critical systems' assets |
| 2-1-1-2 | **Identify asset owners** and involve them in the asset management lifecycle for critical systems |

### 2-2 Identity and Access Management

Extends ECC control 2-2-3. Two main controls:

**2-2-1** — Additional IAM requirements for critical systems:

| Sub-control | Obligation |
|-------------|-----------|
| 2-2-1-1 | **Prohibit remote access from outside the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia** |
| 2-2-1-2 | **Restrict** remote access from inside KSA; **verify each access attempt** via the organisation's SOC; continuously monitor remote access activities |
| 2-2-1-3 | Use **MFA for all users** (not just privileged) |
| 2-2-1-4 | Use **MFA for privileged users** and on the management workstations designated in 2-3-1-4 |
| 2-2-1-5 | Develop and implement a **high-standard, secure password policy** |
| 2-2-1-6 | Utilise **secure methods and hashing algorithms** for storing and processing passwords |
| 2-2-1-7 | **Securely manage service accounts**; disable interactive login from service accounts |
| 2-2-1-8 | **Prohibit direct database access** for all users except DBAs; user interaction with databases must go through applications only; apply security solutions to limit classified data visibility even for DBAs |

**2-2-2** (references ECC 2-2-3-5) — User identities and access rights to critical systems must be reviewed **at least once every three months**.

**Critical audit notes:**
- Remote access from outside KSA is **prohibited outright** (2-2-1-1) — no exception pathway exists.
- MFA is required for **all users** (2-2-1-3), not only privileged accounts or remote access.
- Quarterly access-rights review (2-2-2) is four times more frequent than typical ECC practice.

---

### 2-3 Information System and Information Processing Facilities Protection

Extends ECC control 2-3-3.

| Sub-control | Obligation |
|-------------|-----------|
| 2-3-1-1 | **Application whitelisting** on servers hosting critical systems — only approved executables may run |
| 2-3-1-2 | Protect critical system servers using **organisation-approved endpoint protection solutions** |
| 2-3-1-3 | Apply security patches: **at least monthly** for external/internet-connected critical systems; **at least every three months** for internal critical systems |
| 2-3-1-4 | Allocate specific workstations in an **isolated Management Network** (no email, no internet) for use by highly privileged accounts |
| 2-3-1-5 | **Encrypt all non-console administrative network traffic** using secure encryption algorithms and protocols |
| 2-3-1-6 | Review critical systems' **configurations and hardening at least every six months** |
| 2-3-1-7 | Review and change **default configurations**; ensure removal of hard-coded, backdoor, and default passwords |
| 2-3-1-8 | **Protect system logs and critical files** from unauthorised access, tampering, modification, and deletion |

---

### 2-4 Networks Security Management

Extends ECC control 2-5-3.

| Sub-control | Obligation |
|-------------|-----------|
| 2-4-1-1 | **Logically and/or physically segregate and isolate** critical systems' networks |
| 2-4-1-2 | Review **firewall rules and access lists at least every six months** |
| 2-4-1-3 | **Prohibit direct connection** between local network devices and critical systems unless those devices are scanned and verified to meet critical system security levels |
| 2-4-1-4 | **Prohibit critical systems from connecting to wireless networks** |
| 2-4-1-5 | Deploy **APT protection at the network layer** |
| 2-4-1-6 | **Prohibit internet connection** for critical systems providing internal services with no strong need for external access |
| 2-4-1-7 | Critical systems providing services to a limited number of organisations (not individuals) shall **use networks isolated from the internet** |
| 2-4-1-8 | Protect against **DDoS attacks** |
| 2-4-1-9 | Apply **whitelisting-only** rules to critical systems' firewall access lists |

**Critical audit notes:**
- Wireless prohibition (2-4-1-4) is absolute — no risk-assessment exception.
- Firewall access lists must be **whitelist-only** (2-4-1-9) — any default-allow posture is non-compliant.
- Internet isolation for internal critical systems (2-4-1-6) is a common gap in legacy environments.

### 2-5 Mobile Devices Security

Extends ECC control 2-6-3.

| Sub-control | Obligation |
|-------------|-----------|
| 2-5-1-1 | **Prohibit mobile device access to critical systems** except temporarily after risk assessment and cybersecurity function approval |
| 2-5-1-2 | Implement **full disk encryption** on mobile devices with access to critical systems |

---

### 2-6 Data and Information Protection

Extends ECC control 2-7-3.

| Sub-control | Obligation |
|-------------|-----------|
| 2-6-1-1 | **Prohibit use of critical systems' data in any non-production environment** without strict protective controls (data masking or scrambling) |
| 2-6-1-2 | **Classify all data** within critical systems |
| 2-6-1-3 | Protect classified critical systems data using **DLP techniques** |
| 2-6-1-4 | Identify **retention periods** per relevant legislation; retain only required data in production environments |
| 2-6-1-5 | **Prohibit transfer of critical systems' data** from production to any other environment |

---

### 2-7 Cryptography

Extends ECC control 2-8-3.

| Sub-control | Obligation |
|-------------|-----------|
| 2-7-1-1 | **Encrypt all critical systems' data-in-transit** |
| 2-7-1-2 | **Encrypt all critical systems' data-at-rest** at the level of files, database, or specific database columns |
| 2-7-1-3 | Use **secure and up-to-date methods, algorithms, keys, and devices** per NCA published guidance |

### 2-8 Backup and Recovery Management

Extends ECC control 2-9-3.

| Control | Obligation |
|---------|-----------|
| 2-8-1-1 | **Online and offline backup scope must cover all critical systems** |
| 2-8-1-2 | Perform backups at planned intervals per risk assessment; **NCA recommends daily backups** for critical systems |
| 2-8-1-3 | **Secure access, storage, and transfer** of backups and storage media |
| 2-8-2 | (References ECC 2-9-3-3) Conduct **backup recovery tests at least once every three months** |

### 2-9 Vulnerabilities Management

Extends ECC control 2-10-3.

| Control | Obligation |
|---------|-----------|
| 2-9-1-1 | Use **trusted methods and tools** for vulnerability assessments |
| 2-9-1-2 | Assess and remediate: **at least monthly** for external/internet-connected; **at least every three months** for internal critical systems |
| 2-9-1-3 | **Immediately remediate critical vulnerabilities** per the organisation's change management process |
| 2-9-2 | (References ECC 2-10-3-1) Vulnerability assessments **at least once every month** |

### 2-10 Penetration Testing

Extends ECC control 2-11-3.

| Control | Obligation |
|---------|-----------|
| 2-10-1-1 | Scope must cover **all technical components** and all internal and external services |
| 2-10-1-2 | Conducted by **a qualified team** |
| 2-10-2 | (References ECC 2-11-3-2) **At least once every six months** |

---

### 2-11 Cybersecurity Event Logs and Monitoring Management

Extends ECC control 2-12-3.

| Control | Obligation |
|---------|-----------|
| 2-11-1-1 | Activate event logs on **all technical components** of critical systems |
| 2-11-1-2 | Activate and monitor **file integrity management** alerts and logs |
| 2-11-1-3 | Monitor and analyse **user behaviour (UBA)** |
| 2-11-1-4 | Monitor critical systems security events **around the clock (24/7)** |
| 2-11-1-5 | Maintain and protect logs; include all details (time, date, ID, affected system) |
| 2-11-2 | (References ECC 2-12-3-5) Log **retention period is 18 months minimum** |

**Critical audit notes:**
- 24/7 monitoring (2-11-1-4) requires a staffed or managed SOC — batch/daily log review is non-compliant.
- 18-month log retention (2-11-2) is 50% longer than the ECC baseline; verify SIEM storage capacity.
- UBA (2-11-1-3) requires specific tooling beyond standard log collection.

### 2-12 Web Application Security

Extends ECC control 2-15-3.

| Control | Obligation |
|---------|-----------|
| 2-12-1-1 | Implement **secure session management** — session authenticity, lockout, and timeout |
| 2-12-1-2 | Apply **minimum OWASP Top Ten standards** |
| 2-12-2 | (References ECC 2-15-3-2) Use **multi-tier architecture with minimum 3 tiers** |

### 2-13 Application Security *(new subdomain, no ECC parent)*

Applies to **internal applications** of critical systems.

| Control | Obligation |
|---------|-----------|
| 2-13-1 | Requirements must be **defined, documented, and approved** |
| 2-13-2 | Requirements must be **implemented** |
| 2-13-3 | Requirements must include at minimum: (2-13-3-1) multi-tier architecture ≥3 tiers; (2-13-3-2) secure protocols (e.g. HTTPS); (2-13-3-3) acceptable use policy for users; (2-13-3-4) secure session management |
| 2-13-4 | Requirements must be **reviewed periodically** |

## Domain 3 — Cybersecurity Resilience (1 subdomain)

### 3-1 Cybersecurity Resilience Aspects of BCM

Extends ECC control 3-1-3.

| Sub-control | Obligation |
|-------------|-----------|
| 3-1-1-1 | **Establish a disaster recovery centre** for critical systems |
| 3-1-1-2 | **Incorporate critical systems** within disaster recovery plans |
| 3-1-1-3 | Conduct **DR plan tests at least annually** |

## Domain 4 — Third-Party and Cloud Computing Cybersecurity (2 subdomains)

### 4-1 Third-Party Cybersecurity

Extends ECC subdomain 4-1.

| Sub-control | Obligation |
|-------------|-----------|
| 4-1-1-1 | **Screen or vet** outsourcing/managed service companies and their personnel working on critical systems |
| 4-1-1-2 | Outsourcing and managed services of critical systems must **rely on Saudi companies and organisations**, per relevant legislative and regulatory requirements |

**Critical compliance note:** 4-1-1-2 is a hard national sourcing requirement — not a preference. Foreign-headquartered MSPs are non-compliant even with KSA-based staff unless the contracting entity itself is a Saudi company.

---

### 4-2 Cloud Computing and Hosting Cybersecurity

Extends ECC control 4-2-3. Applies only where cloud/hosting is used for critical systems.

| Sub-control | Obligation |
|-------------|-----------|
| 4-2-1-1 | Hosting of critical systems and **any part of their technical components** must be inside the organisation **or** within cloud services provided by **government organisations or Saudi companies** that are in compliance with NCA's **CCC**, taking into account the classification of hosted data |

**Practical note:** Public hyperscale cloud is not automatically compliant — the CSP must be a Saudi company or government entity with CCC compliance. Even then, data classification must be considered.

## Audit hot spots — triage list

1. **No formal critical system designation register:** Designation must be documented before any other compliance step.
2. **Remote access from outside KSA (2-2-1-1):** Any active capability is a clear non-compliance — no exception pathway.
3. **MFA for all users (2-2-1-3):** Must apply to every user, not only admins.
4. **Monthly risk register review (1-2-1-2):** Organisations apply ECC annual cadence and miss the monthly CSCC requirement.
5. **Application whitelisting (2-3-1-1):** Default-allow endpoint posture is non-compliant.
6. **Wireless prohibition (2-4-1-4):** Absolute — no risk-assessment exception.
7. **Firewall whitelist-only access lists (2-4-1-9):** Default-deny with explicit permit required; semi-annual review evidence needed (2-4-1-2).
8. **Management Network isolation (2-3-1-4):** Shared-use admin machines with email/internet access are non-compliant.
9. **18-month log retention (2-11-2):** Verify SIEM storage capacity — 90-day or 12-month configurations are non-compliant.
10. **24/7 monitoring (2-11-1-4):** Requires continuous SOC coverage; batch daily review is non-compliant.
11. **DR centre designation (3-1-1-1):** Must exist as a dedicated facility; confirm DR plan and annual test evidence.
12. **Saudi outsourcing requirement (4-1-1-2):** Check all outsourcing and managed service contracts.
13. **Critical system hosting restriction (4-2-1-1):** CSP must be a Saudi company or government entity with CCC compliance.
14. **Quarterly IAM review (2-2-2):** Four times per year required; most organisations conduct annual reviews.
15. **Source code security (1-3-2):** Code review, source code access controls, API security, and test-to-production hygiene are mandatory and frequently undocumented.
16. **Semi-annual penetration tests (2-10-2):** Annual testing is non-compliant for critical systems.
17. **Quarterly backup recovery tests (2-8-2):** Annual recovery tests are insufficient.

## Common pitfalls

- Scoping critical systems too narrowly — failing to designate systems that meet one or more of the seven criteria.
- Treating ECC compliance as sufficient — CSCC adds significant obligations on top.
- Applying ECC review cadences (annual IAM review, annual pen test) when CSCC requires quarterly or semi-annual frequencies.
- Configuring MFA only for admin/privileged users — CSCC requires it for all users.
- Maintaining any remote access pathway from outside KSA, even emergency or vendor support channels.
- Assuming a non-Saudi MSP is compliant because it has KSA-based engineers — the contracting entity must be a Saudi company.
- Using public hyperscale cloud without confirming CCC compliance and Saudi company status.
- Meeting ECC's 12-month log retention without extending to 18 months for critical system logs.
- Missing Application Security (2-13) entirely — it is a new CSCC subdomain with no ECC parent.
- Treating backup recovery testing as annual — quarterly evidence required.

## Key definitions (from CSCC Terms)

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Critical Vulnerabilities** | Vulnerabilities that if exploited could lead to unauthorised access to data, information, devices, or systems |
| **Data-At-Rest** | Inactive data stored in permanent storage media |
| **Data-In-Transit** | Data transmitted between locations over any network |
| **Data Leakage Prevention** | Methods to prevent data from circulating outside the organisation regardless of form or location |
| **Data Masking** | Hiding part of data by replacing characters/values with symbols |
| **Data Scrambling** | Rearranging/replacing data in a dataset so values are inconsistent with original records and cannot be restored |
| **Screening or Vetting** | Verifying the identity of persons prior to employment due to their expected association with sensitive systems |
| **Secret** | Data classification level where unauthorised disclosure causes severe damage to national security, economy, or KSA's international relationships |
| **Top Secret** | Data classification level where unauthorised disclosure causes severe damage and recovery is hardly possible |
| **Stress Testing** | Deliberately intense testing to determine system stability by overwhelming its resources |
| **User Behavior Analytics (UBA)** | Tracking, collecting, and analysing user data to identify patterns and detect harmful or unusual behaviour |

# NDMO — Data Management and Personal Data Protection Standards

## At a glance

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Issuer | National Data Management Office (NDMO), under SDAIA |
| Document set | National Data Management & Personal Data Protection Standards |
| Current version | v1.5 (2022) — confirm against NDMO portal for updates |
| Applicability | Government and public-sector entities in KSA; private sector in regulated contexts and where contracted by government |
| Companion | PDPL (Royal Decree M/19) and SDAIA Implementing Regulations |

## Purpose

NDMO Standards operationalise data management and personal-data protection across the Saudi public sector. They translate policy into a measurable set of expectations for data governance, classification, quality, interoperability, security, and privacy in government bodies.

## Domain coverage

NDMO Standards span 15 data-management domains. The user should expect to encounter the following groupings (NDMO uses its own labelling — confirm exact titles in the published version):

1. Data Governance
2. Data Catalogue and Metadata
3. Data Architecture, Modelling, and Design
4. Data Storage and Operations
5. Data Quality
6. Data Integration and Interoperability
7. Document and Content Management
8. Reference and Master Data Management
9. Data Warehousing and Business Intelligence
10. Data Sharing
11. Data Classification (aligned to the four-level KSA scheme)
12. Open Data
13. Freedom of Information
14. Personal Data Protection
15. Data Security (with cross-reference to NCA controls)

## Personal Data Protection Standards within NDMO

These re-state and operationalise PDPL obligations specifically for government entities. They cover:

- Lawful basis for processing in a public-sector context (often statutory)
- Privacy notices in government services
- Data-subject rights handling
- Data-protection impact assessments
- DPO designation in government bodies
- Breach notification through the government channel
- Cross-border transfer for government data (typically tighter than private-sector)
- Records of processing aligned to the national data catalogue

## Classification

NDMO mandates the four-level Saudi government classification:

1. Top Secret
2. Secret
3. Confidential
4. Public

Each level drives:

- Permitted recipients
- Permitted storage and processing locations (in-Kingdom for higher tiers)
- Encryption and key custody
- Retention and disposal
- Sharing approvals required (often via specific sharing agreement and NDMO oversight)

## Open data

Public-sector entities are expected to publish certain datasets as Open Data under NDMO's open-data programme, subject to classification and privacy review. The Open Data Standards specify metadata, licence, format, and update cadence.

## Audit hot spots

- No data office or designated chief data officer in the entity
- Data catalogue absent or outdated
- Classification scheme adopted in policy but not applied to data assets in practice
- Data sharing with other public bodies without a sharing agreement
- Personal data processed by government services without a privacy notice on the citizen-facing channel
- Open Data datasets containing residual personal data
- DPIAs not produced for new digital services

## Required artefacts

- Data management policy and operating model
- Data catalogue / metadata register
- Classified data inventory
- Data-quality KPIs and reporting
- Data-sharing register and agreements
- Privacy notices on citizen services
- Records of processing
- DPIA register
- Open Data publication evidence (where applicable)
- Designation of Data Office / Chief Data Officer / DPO

## Cross-references

- **PDPL** + Implementing Regulations + Transfer Regulation
- **NCA ECC-2:2024** for baseline cybersecurity
- **NCA DCC-1:2022** for data cybersecurity
- **NCA OTCC-1:2022** where the entity operates OT
- **CST CCRF** where government data is held in commercial cloud

## Verification note

NDMO publishes updates and clarifications periodically. Domain numbering and specific control statements should be confirmed against the latest published Standards, not relied on from memory. The interplay between NDMO and SDAIA on PDPL supervision can also evolve — verify the current allocation of supervisory roles for the user's entity type before formal output.
